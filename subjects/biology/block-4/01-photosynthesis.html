Photosynthesis Introduction

How Producers Make Their Own Food

Plants and other producers make their own food through a process called photosynthesis. This process happens in the leaves and other parts of the plant that are above the ground.

The word photosynthesis comes from two parts: photo (meaning light) and synthesis (meaning to make). Photosynthesis can only happen when there is light.

  • Leaves are made up of cells
  • Photosynthesis takes place inside plant cells
  • Light transfers energy from the light source to the leaf cells
  • This provides the energy needed for photosynthesis to take place

Reactants and Products of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is a series of chemical reactions that happen inside leaf cells. During chemical reactions, one or more substances are broken down (the reactants) and one or more new substances are made (the products).

WORD EQUATION

water + carbon dioxide → glucose + oxygen

The Role of Each Substance

Substance Role Where It Comes From / Goes
Water Reactant Taken in through the roots and transported to the leaves
Carbon dioxide Reactant Taken in from the air through the leaves
Glucose Product Made inside leaf cells, transported to all parts of the plant
Oxygen Product Released into the air from the leaves
Light Energy source (NOT a reactant) Provides the energy needed for the chemical reactions

Important: Light is not a substance and is not a chemical reactant. Light provides the energy needed for the chemical reactions of photosynthesis to take place.

Producers and Photosynthesis

All living organisms need food to stay alive and grow. This is the life process of nutrition. Some organisms are producers that make their own food in their cells using photosynthesis.

Examples of producers include:

  • Plants that grow on land (grasses, trees, flowers)
  • Aquatic plants that grow in water
  • Microorganisms such as algae

The reactants and products of photosynthesis are all made up of just three elements: carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen.

A large tree can produce around 90 kg of glucose from photosynthesis in a single sunny day. Most of the mass of that glucose is made up of carbon atoms that came from carbon dioxide gas in the air.

Uses of Glucose

The glucose made by photosynthesis is used by producers in several ways:

Use Details
Fuel for cellular respiration Transfers energy for life processes
Building biomass Used for growth and repair of cells and tissues
Making larger carbohydrates Starch (for storage), cellulose and lignin (for cell walls)
Making lipids Fats and oils for storage and cell membranes
Making amino acids and proteins Combined with nitrogen (from nitrate mineral ions) to make amino acids, proteins, and enzymes
KEY TERM

Biomass is all the biological material that makes up the cells and tissues of an organism.

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

Write the word equation for photosynthesis.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • water + carbon dioxide → glucose + oxygen (1 mark for reactants, 1 mark for products)
Question 2 [2 marks]

Explain why light is needed for photosynthesis but is not a reactant.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Light is not a substance / not a chemical (1 mark)
  • Light provides the energy needed for the chemical reactions of photosynthesis (1 mark)
Question 3 [3 marks]

Explain why moving a houseplant from a dim corner to a window will help it grow bigger.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The plant will receive more light from the Sun (1 mark)
  • Light provides energy for photosynthesis, which makes the plant's food (glucose) (1 mark)
  • This food is used as a source of materials for growth / energy for life processes (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

State two ways in which glucose produced by photosynthesis is used by a plant.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Any two from: fuel for cellular respiration / building biomass for growth / making starch for storage / making cellulose for cell walls / making lipids / making amino acids and proteins (1 mark each)
Question 5 [3 marks]

Name the three elements that make up all the reactants and products of photosynthesis. For each element, name one reactant or product that contains it.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Carbon - found in carbon dioxide / glucose (1 mark)
  • Hydrogen - found in water / glucose (1 mark)
  • Oxygen - found in water / carbon dioxide / glucose / oxygen gas (1 mark)
