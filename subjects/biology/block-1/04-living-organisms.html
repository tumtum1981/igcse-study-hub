B1.4.1

Characteristics of Living Organisms

Summary

All living organisms share seven characteristics that distinguish them from non-living things. These can be remembered using the mnemonic MRS GREN or MRS NERG.

The Seven Life Processes

Letter Process Definition
M Movement Changing position or place - can be the whole organism or parts within it
R Respiration Chemical reactions that break down nutrients to release energy
S Sensitivity Detecting and responding to changes in the environment (stimuli)
G Growth Permanent increase in size and dry mass
R Reproduction Producing offspring (new individuals of the same species)
E Excretion Removing toxic waste products of metabolism from the body
N Nutrition Taking in materials for energy, growth, and development

Important Definitions

Respiration vs Breathing

  • Respiration is a chemical reaction that releases energy from glucose
  • Breathing is the physical process of moving air in and out of lungs
  • All living things respire, but not all breathe (plants don't have lungs)

Excretion vs Egestion

  • Excretion is removing waste products of metabolism (e.g., CO2, urea)
  • Egestion is removing undigested food (faeces) - not a metabolic waste

Examples in Different Organisms

Process Animals Plants
Movement Walking, running, swimming Growth towards light, opening/closing stomata
Nutrition Eating food (heterotrophs) Photosynthesis (autotrophs)
Excretion CO2, urea, water O2, CO2 (depending on conditions)
Sensitivity Eyes, ears, touch Detecting light direction

Key Points to Remember

  • All living things show all seven characteristics
  • MRS GREN helps you remember them
  • Respiration happens in all living cells
  • Excretion is not the same as egestion
  • Plants move but in different ways to animals
