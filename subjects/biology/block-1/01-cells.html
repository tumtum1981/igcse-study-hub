Cells: The Building Blocks of Life

Summary

All living organisms are made up of cells. Cells are the basic structural and functional units of life. There are two main types of cells: eukaryotic cells (found in plants, animals, and fungi) and prokaryotic cells (found in bacteria).

Animal Cell Structure

Animal cells contain several key structures, each with a specific function:

Structure Function
Nucleus Contains genetic material (DNA) and controls cell activities
Cytoplasm Jelly-like substance where chemical reactions occur
Cell membrane Controls what enters and leaves the cell (partially permeable)
Mitochondria Site of aerobic respiration - releases energy from glucose
Ribosomes Site of protein synthesis

Plant Cell Structure

Plant cells have all the structures found in animal cells, plus three additional features:

Structure Function
Cell wall Made of cellulose - provides support and prevents the cell from bursting
Chloroplasts Contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis - absorb light energy
Large permanent vacuole Contains cell sap - keeps the cell turgid (firm)

Comparing Animal and Plant Cells

Feature Animal Cell Plant Cell
Cell wall No Yes (cellulose)
Chloroplasts No Yes (in green parts)
Vacuole Small or none Large, permanent
Nucleus Yes Yes
Mitochondria Yes Yes

Key Points to Remember

  • All living things are made of cells
  • The nucleus controls the cell and contains DNA
  • Mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration
  • Plant cells have a cell wall, chloroplasts, and large vacuole
  • The cell membrane controls what enters and leaves
