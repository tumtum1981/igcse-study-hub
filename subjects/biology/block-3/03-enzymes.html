B3.3.1

Enzymes

Summary

Enzymes are biological catalysts made of protein. They speed up chemical reactions in living organisms without being used up. Each enzyme has a specific shape and only works on one type of substrate - this is called enzyme specificity.

What are Enzymes?

  • Enzymes are proteins that act as biological catalysts
  • They speed up reactions without being changed or used up
  • They work inside cells (intracellular) and outside cells (extracellular)
  • Each enzyme only catalyses one specific reaction

The Lock and Key Model

The lock and key model explains how enzymes work:

  • Each enzyme has an active site with a specific shape
  • Only substrates with the correct shape can fit into the active site
  • The substrate binds to the active site forming an enzyme-substrate complex
  • The reaction occurs and products are released
  • The enzyme is unchanged and can be used again

This explains specificity - why each enzyme only works on one substrate.

Factors Affecting Enzyme Activity

Temperature

  • As temperature increases, enzyme activity increases (more kinetic energy, more collisions)
  • Each enzyme has an optimum temperature where it works best (usually around 37Â°C for human enzymes)
  • Above the optimum, the enzyme denatures - the active site changes shape permanently
  • Denatured enzymes cannot bind to substrates

pH

  • Each enzyme has an optimum pH where it works best
  • Too high or too low pH causes the enzyme to denature
  • Different enzymes have different optimum pH values:
Enzyme Location Optimum pH
Pepsin (protease) Stomach pH 2 (acidic)
Salivary amylase Mouth pH 7 (neutral)
Pancreatic enzymes Small intestine pH 8 (alkaline)

Denaturation

When an enzyme is denatured:

  • The bonds holding its shape break
  • The active site changes shape permanently
  • The substrate can no longer fit
  • The enzyme can no longer catalyse the reaction

Denaturation is caused by high temperatures or extreme pH values. It is permanent and cannot be reversed.

Digestive Enzymes

Enzyme Substrate Product Where Produced
Amylase Starch Maltose/glucose Salivary glands, pancreas
Protease Protein Amino acids Stomach, pancreas
Lipase Lipids Fatty acids + glycerol Pancreas

Key Points to Remember

  • Enzymes are protein catalysts that speed up reactions
  • The lock and key model explains enzyme specificity
  • Each enzyme has an optimum temperature and pH
  • Denaturation permanently changes the active site shape
  • Enzymes are not used up and can be reused
