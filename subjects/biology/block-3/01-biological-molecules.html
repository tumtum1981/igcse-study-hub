B3.1.1

Biological Molecules

Summary

Living organisms are made up of biological molecules. The four main groups are carbohydrates, proteins, lipids (fats and oils), and nucleic acids. Each type of molecule has specific functions in the body and can be identified using food tests.

The Four Main Biological Molecules

Molecule Type Made From Function Examples
Carbohydrates Carbon, hydrogen, oxygen Energy source and storage Glucose, starch, cellulose
Proteins Amino acids Growth, repair, enzymes Enzymes, antibodies, haemoglobin
Lipids Fatty acids and glycerol Energy storage, insulation Fats, oils
Nucleic acids Nucleotides Genetic information DNA, RNA

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are made from simple sugars (monosaccharides) like glucose. They can be classified into three types:

  • Monosaccharides: Single sugar units (e.g., glucose, fructose)
  • Disaccharides: Two sugar units joined together (e.g., sucrose, maltose)
  • Polysaccharides: Many sugar units joined together (e.g., starch, glycogen, cellulose)

Starch is the storage carbohydrate in plants. Glycogen is the storage carbohydrate in animals, stored in the liver and muscles.

Proteins

Proteins are made from long chains of amino acids. There are 20 different amino acids that can be combined in different orders to make thousands of different proteins.

Proteins have many functions:

  • Enzymes: Biological catalysts that speed up reactions
  • Structural: Collagen in skin, keratin in hair
  • Transport: Haemoglobin carries oxygen in blood
  • Defence: Antibodies fight pathogens
  • Hormones: Insulin controls blood glucose

Lipids (Fats and Oils)

Lipids are made from glycerol and three fatty acids. They contain more energy per gram than carbohydrates.

Functions of lipids:

  • Long-term energy storage
  • Insulation (keeping warm)
  • Protection of organs
  • Making cell membranes

Fats are solid at room temperature (mainly from animals). Oils are liquid at room temperature (mainly from plants).

Food Tests

Nutrient Test Positive Result
Starch Iodine solution Brown/orange to blue-black
Reducing sugars (glucose) Benedict's test (heat) Blue to green/yellow/orange/brick red
Protein Biuret test Blue to purple/lilac
Lipids Ethanol emulsion test Cloudy white emulsion

Key Points to Remember

  • Carbohydrates provide quick energy; starch stores energy in plants
  • Proteins are made of amino acids and have many functions
  • Lipids store more energy per gram than carbohydrates
  • Each nutrient has a specific food test with a colour change
  • Benedict's test requires heating; the colour shows sugar concentration
