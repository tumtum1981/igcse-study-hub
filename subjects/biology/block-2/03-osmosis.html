B2.3.1

Osmosis

Summary

Osmosis is the movement of water molecules through a partially permeable membrane from a region of higher water potential to a region of lower water potential.

Definition

Osmosis
The net movement of water molecules from a region of higher water potential (dilute solution) to a region of lower water potential (concentrated solution), through a partially permeable membrane

Key Terms

Term Meaning
Partially permeable membrane A membrane that allows some molecules through but not others
Water potential The tendency of water to move - pure water has the highest water potential
Dilute solution Low solute concentration, high water potential
Concentrated solution High solute concentration, low water potential

Effects on Animal Cells

Solution Water Movement Effect on Cell
Pure water (hypotonic) Water enters cell Cell swells and may burst (lysis)
Same concentration (isotonic) No net movement Cell stays the same
Concentrated solution (hypertonic) Water leaves cell Cell shrinks (crenation)

Effects on Plant Cells

Solution Water Movement Effect on Cell
Pure water (hypotonic) Water enters cell Cell becomes turgid (firm) - cell wall prevents bursting
Same concentration (isotonic) No net movement Cell stays the same
Concentrated solution (hypertonic) Water leaves cell Cell becomes plasmolysed - membrane pulls away from wall

Key Points to Remember

  • Osmosis is a special type of diffusion - only water moves
  • Water moves from HIGH water potential to LOW water potential
  • Plant cells don't burst because of the cell wall
  • Turgid cells help support plants
  • Plasmolysis causes wilting
