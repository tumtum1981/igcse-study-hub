B2.2.1

Diffusion

Summary

Diffusion is the net movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration. It is a passive process - no energy is required.

Definition

Diffusion
The net movement of particles from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration, down a concentration gradient

Key Features of Diffusion

  • Passive process - no energy required
  • Particles move down the concentration gradient
  • Continues until equilibrium is reached (equal concentration everywhere)
  • Particles still move at equilibrium, but no net movement

Factors Affecting Rate of Diffusion

Factor Effect on Rate
Concentration gradient Greater difference = faster diffusion
Temperature Higher temperature = faster diffusion (more kinetic energy)
Surface area Larger surface area = faster diffusion
Distance Shorter distance = faster diffusion

Examples of Diffusion in Living Organisms

Example Substance Direction
Gas exchange in lungs Oxygen Alveoli to blood
Gas exchange in lungs Carbon dioxide Blood to alveoli
Absorption in intestine Glucose, amino acids Gut to blood
Photosynthesis in leaves Carbon dioxide Air to leaf cells

Key Points to Remember

  • Diffusion is passive (no energy needed)
  • Movement is from HIGH to LOW concentration
  • Faster with: higher temperature, larger surface area, steeper gradient
  • Essential for gas exchange and nutrient absorption
