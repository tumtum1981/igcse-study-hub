B2.5.1

Investigating Factors that Affect Diffusion

Summary

We can investigate diffusion by observing what happens when a coloured jelly cube is placed in acid. The acid particles diffuse into the permeable jelly, causing it to turn colourless. By changing different variables (such as temperature or cube size), we can study what factors affect the rate of diffusion.

Diffusion into a Jelly Cube

A cube of coloured jelly is a permeable material - liquids are able to move through it. When we place the jelly cube into acid:

  • There is a high concentration of acid particles outside the cube
  • The net movement of acid particles is down the concentration gradient - from outside to inside the cube
  • Particles of acid move into the cube by diffusion from each surface towards the middle
  • The acid causes the coloured jelly to turn colourless, starting from the surfaces and moving inwards

Scientific Variables

In a science experiment, a variable is a factor that can be changed, kept the same, or measured.

Type of Variable Description
Independent variable The factor that is being changed (what you choose to vary)
Dependent variable The factor that is being measured (the outcome you observe)
Control variables Factors that are kept the same, so only the independent variable affects the results

Scientific Predictions

Before doing an experiment, we make a scientific prediction - a testable statement about a possible outcome. A prediction states the effect that changing the independent variable will have on the dependent variable.

Type of Statement Example
Explanation (not a prediction) Diffusion is caused by moving particles
Observation (not a prediction) The jelly cube turned colourless in 13 minutes 45 seconds
Prediction Using stronger acid will make the jelly cube turn colourless faster

Experiment 1: Changing Temperature

Investigating how temperature affects the rate of diffusion of acid into jelly cubes:

Variable Detail
Independent variable Temperature of the acid (20°C, 40°C, 60°C)
Dependent variable Time taken to decolourise each cube
Control variables Size of the cubes, concentration of acid, type of jelly

Experiment 2: Changing Cube Size

Investigating how the size of the jelly cube affects the time taken for diffusion:

Variable Detail
Independent variable Size of the cubes (1.0 cm, 1.5 cm, 2.0 cm width)
Dependent variable Time taken to decolourise each cube
Control variables Temperature of the acid, concentration of acid, type of jelly

Prediction: The time taken to decolourise the cube will be longer for a cube with a larger width, because the acid has further to diffuse from the surface to the centre.

Key Points to Remember

  • Diffusion is the net movement of particles down a concentration gradient
  • Jelly is permeable - acid particles can diffuse into it
  • The independent variable is what you change; the dependent variable is what you measure
  • Control variables must be kept the same to make a fair test
  • A scientific prediction is a testable statement about the expected outcome
  • Higher temperature increases the rate of diffusion (particles move faster)
  • Larger cubes take longer to decolourise (particles must travel further)
