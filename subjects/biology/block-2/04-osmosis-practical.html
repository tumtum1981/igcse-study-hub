B2.4.1

Osmosis Practical Investigation

Summary

The osmosis practical investigates how different concentrations of solution affect the mass or length of potato (or other plant tissue) samples. This demonstrates osmosis in action.

Method

  1. Cut potato cylinders of equal size using a cork borer
  2. Measure and record the initial mass (or length) of each cylinder
  3. Place cylinders in different concentrations of sucrose/salt solution
  4. Leave for a set time (e.g., 30 minutes)
  5. Remove, blot dry, and measure final mass
  6. Calculate percentage change in mass

Calculating Percentage Change

% Change = (Final - Initial) / Initial x 100
A positive value means mass increased (water entered by osmosis)

Expected Results

Solution Mass Change Explanation
Pure water (0%) Increase Water enters potato by osmosis
Dilute solution Small increase Some water enters
Isotonic solution No change No net water movement
Concentrated solution Decrease Water leaves potato by osmosis

Control Variables

  • Same size potato cylinders
  • Same temperature throughout
  • Same time in solution
  • Same volume of solution
  • Same type of potato

Key Points to Remember

  • Blot dry before weighing to remove surface water
  • Use percentage change to compare fairly
  • The isotonic point is where the line crosses zero
  • Plot concentration on x-axis, % change on y-axis
