Size of Cells and Surface Area to Volume Ratio

Summary

Cells need to exchange substances with their environment. The surface area to volume ratio (SA:V) affects how efficiently they can do this. Smaller cells have a larger SA:V ratio, making exchange more efficient.

Surface Area to Volume Ratio

SA:V Ratio = Surface Area / Volume
As objects get larger, their volume increases faster than their surface area
Cube Side Length Surface Area Volume SA:V Ratio
1 cm 6 cm2 1 cm3 6:1
2 cm 24 cm2 8 cm3 3:1
3 cm 54 cm2 27 cm3 2:1

Why Cell Size Matters

  • Small cells have a large SA:V ratio - substances can diffuse in/out quickly
  • Large cells have a small SA:V ratio - the centre of the cell is far from the surface
  • Cells cannot grow too large because diffusion would be too slow
  • This is why large organisms have many small cells, not a few large ones

Adaptations to Increase Surface Area

Organisms have evolved structures to increase their surface area for exchange:

Structure Location Purpose
Villi and microvilli Small intestine Increase surface area for nutrient absorption
Alveoli Lungs Increase surface area for gas exchange
Root hair cells Plant roots Increase surface area for water absorption

Key Points to Remember

  • SA:V ratio decreases as size increases
  • Small cells are more efficient at exchange
  • Organisms have adaptations to increase surface area
  • Diffusion is more efficient with a large SA:V ratio

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

Explain why cells cannot grow very large.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • As cells get larger, the SA:V ratio decreases (1 mark)
  • Diffusion would be too slow to supply the cell's needs (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

Calculate the surface area to volume ratio of a cube with sides of 4 cm.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Surface area = 6 x 4 x 4 = 96 cm2 (1 mark)
  • Volume = 4 x 4 x 4 = 64 cm3 (1 mark)
  • SA:V = 96:64 = 1.5:1 (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Name one structure in the human body that increases surface area and state its function.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Villi - increase surface area for absorption in the small intestine (1 mark each)
  • OR Alveoli - increase surface area for gas exchange in lungs
