C2.5.1

Giant Covalent Structures

Summary

Giant covalent structures (also called macromolecular structures) are large networks of atoms joined by covalent bonds. Unlike simple covalent molecules, they have very high melting points because many strong covalent bonds must be broken.

Diamond

Property Explanation
Structure Each carbon atom bonded to 4 others in a tetrahedral arrangement
Very hard Strong covalent bonds in all directions - no weak points
Very high melting point Many strong covalent bonds to break
Does not conduct electricity All electrons are used in bonding - no free electrons

Uses: Cutting tools, drill bits, jewellery

Graphite

Property Explanation
Structure Carbon atoms in flat hexagonal layers, each atom bonded to 3 others
Soft and slippery Weak forces between layers - layers can slide over each other
Very high melting point Strong covalent bonds within layers
Conducts electricity Each carbon only uses 3 electrons for bonding - 1 delocalised electron per atom can move along layers

Uses: Pencil leads, lubricants, electrodes

Silicon Dioxide (Silica)

Property Explanation
Structure Each silicon atom bonded to 4 oxygen atoms; each oxygen bonded to 2 silicon atoms
Very hard Strong covalent bonds throughout the structure
Very high melting point Many strong covalent bonds to break
Does not conduct electricity No free electrons or ions

Uses: Glass, sand, quartz

Comparing Diamond and Graphite

Property Diamond Graphite
Bonds per carbon 4 3
Structure 3D tetrahedral 2D layers
Hardness Very hard Soft
Conductivity Does not conduct Conducts
Free electrons None 1 per carbon atom

Key Points to Remember

  • Giant covalent structures have high melting points due to many strong bonds
  • Diamond: 4 bonds per carbon, very hard, doesn't conduct
  • Graphite: 3 bonds per carbon, layers slide, conducts electricity
  • Both diamond and graphite are forms of pure carbon (allotropes)

Question 1 [2 marks]

Explain why diamond has a very high melting point.

Question 2 [2 marks]

Explain why graphite can conduct electricity but diamond cannot.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • In graphite, each carbon only bonds to 3 others, leaving 1 delocalised electron (1 mark)
  • In diamond, all 4 electrons are used in bonding - no free electrons (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain why graphite is soft and slippery.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Graphite has layers (1 mark)
  • Weak forces between layers allow them to slide over each other (1 mark)
