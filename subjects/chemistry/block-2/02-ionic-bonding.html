Ionic Bonding

Summary

Ionic bonding occurs when electrons are transferred from a metal atom to a non-metal atom. This creates ions with opposite charges that attract each other through strong electrostatic forces. Ionic compounds form giant ionic lattices with specific properties.

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Ionic Bond The strong electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions
Ion An atom that has gained or lost electrons to form a charged particle
Ionic Lattice A giant regular arrangement of positive and negative ions held together by electrostatic forces
Electrostatic Force The force of attraction between opposite charges (or repulsion between like charges)

How Ionic Bonds Form

  • Ionic bonds form between metals and non-metals
  • Metal atoms lose electrons to form positive ions (cations)
  • Non-metal atoms gain electrons to form negative ions (anions)
  • Both ions achieve a full outer shell (stable electron configuration)
  • The opposite charges attract, holding the ions together

Example: Sodium Chloride (NaCl)

Sodium atom (Na): 2.8.1

Loses 1 electron to become Na+ (2.8)

Chlorine atom (Cl): 2.8.7

Gains 1 electron to become Cl- (2.8.8)

  • The sodium ion Na+ has a +1 charge (11 protons, 10 electrons)
  • The chloride ion Cl- has a -1 charge (17 protons, 18 electrons)
  • Strong electrostatic attraction holds the ions together

Example: Magnesium Oxide (MgO)

Magnesium atom (Mg): 2.8.2

Loses 2 electrons to become Mg2+ (2.8)

Oxygen atom (O): 2.6

Gains 2 electrons to become O2- (2.8)

  • Magnesium loses 2 electrons, oxygen gains 2 electrons
  • Both ions have full outer shells
  • The 2+ and 2- charges create stronger attraction than in NaCl

Properties of Ionic Compounds

Property Explanation
High melting and boiling points Strong electrostatic forces between ions require a lot of energy to overcome
Hard but brittle Rigid lattice structure; when layers shift, like charges repel and the crystal shatters
Do not conduct electricity when solid Ions are held in fixed positions and cannot move
Conduct electricity when molten or dissolved Ions are free to move and carry charge
Soluble in water Water molecules can separate the ions from the lattice

Common Ionic Compounds

Compound Formula Ions Present
Sodium chloride NaCl Na+ and Cl-
Magnesium oxide MgO Mg2+ and O2-
Calcium chloride CaCl2 Ca2+ and 2Cl-
Sodium oxide Na2O 2Na+ and O2-

Key Points to Remember

  • Ionic bonds form between metals and non-metals
  • Electrons are TRANSFERRED from metal to non-metal
  • Both ions achieve stable full outer shells
  • Ionic compounds form giant lattice structures
  • They conduct electricity only when molten or dissolved (ions can move)
