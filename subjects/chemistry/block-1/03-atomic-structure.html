Atomic Structure

Summary

Atoms are made up of three subatomic particles: protons, neutrons, and electrons. Protons and neutrons are found in the nucleus at the centre of the atom, while electrons orbit around the nucleus in shells.

Subatomic Particles

Particle Relative Mass Relative Charge Location
Proton 1 +1 Nucleus
Neutron 1 0 Nucleus
Electron 1/1840 (negligible) -1 Shells around nucleus

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Atomic Number (Z) The number of protons in an atom (also equals the number of electrons in a neutral atom)
Mass Number (A) The total number of protons and neutrons in an atom
Nucleus The dense central part of an atom containing protons and neutrons

Important Formulas

Number of Neutrons = Mass Number - Atomic Number

N = A - Z

In a neutral atom:

Number of Electrons = Number of Protons = Atomic Number

Atomic Notation

Atoms are represented using nuclear notation:

For example, Carbon-12: 126C

The top number (12) is the mass number

The bottom number (6) is the atomic number

  • Carbon has 6 protons (atomic number = 6)
  • Carbon has 6 neutrons (12 - 6 = 6)
  • A neutral carbon atom has 6 electrons

Key Points to Remember

  • The atomic number identifies the element
  • Atoms are neutral because protons (+) equal electrons (-)
  • Almost all the mass is in the nucleus (protons + neutrons)
  • The nucleus is very small compared to the atom (most of an atom is empty space)
  • Elements are arranged in the periodic table by atomic number

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

State the relative mass and relative charge of a proton, neutron, and electron.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Proton: relative mass 1, relative charge +1 (1 mark)
  • Neutron: relative mass 1, relative charge 0 (1 mark)
  • Electron: relative mass 1/1840 (negligible), relative charge -1 (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

An atom has a mass number of 23 and an atomic number of 11. Calculate the number of protons, neutrons, and electrons in this atom.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Protons = 11 (same as atomic number) (1 mark)
  • Neutrons = 23 - 11 = 12 (1 mark)
  • Electrons = 11 (same as protons in neutral atom) (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain why atoms are electrically neutral.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Atoms have equal numbers of protons and electrons (1 mark)
  • Positive charges (+1) from protons cancel out negative charges (-1) from electrons (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

Why is almost all the mass of an atom concentrated in the nucleus?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The nucleus contains protons and neutrons which have a relative mass of 1 (1 mark)
  • Electrons have negligible mass (1/1840) (1 mark)
