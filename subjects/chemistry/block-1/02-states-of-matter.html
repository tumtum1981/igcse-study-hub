States of Matter

Summary

Matter exists in three states: solid, liquid, and gas. The state depends on the arrangement and movement of particles, which is determined by temperature and pressure.

Properties of the Three States

Property Solid Liquid Gas
Particle arrangement Regular, close together Random, close together Random, far apart
Particle movement Vibrate in fixed positions Move around each other Move quickly in all directions
Shape Fixed Takes shape of container Fills container
Volume Fixed Fixed Variable (can be compressed)
Density High High Low

Changes of State

Change From To Energy
Melting Solid Liquid Energy absorbed
Freezing Liquid Solid Energy released
Boiling/Evaporation Liquid Gas Energy absorbed
Condensation Gas Liquid Energy released
Sublimation Solid Gas Energy absorbed

Kinetic Theory

  • All matter is made of particles that are constantly moving
  • Higher temperature = more kinetic energy = faster movement
  • Changes of state occur when enough energy is supplied to overcome forces between particles
  • During a change of state, temperature remains constant (energy goes into breaking bonds)

Key Points to Remember

  • Solids have fixed shape and volume
  • Liquids have fixed volume but take the shape of the container
  • Gases fill their container and can be compressed
  • Changes of state are physical changes (reversible)
  • Particles have more energy in gas > liquid > solid
