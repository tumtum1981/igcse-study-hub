Conservation of Mass

Summary

The law of conservation of mass states that mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. The total mass of the reactants always equals the total mass of the products. This is because atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed.

The Law of Conservation of Mass

In any chemical reaction:

  • No atoms are created or destroyed
  • Atoms are rearranged to form new substances
  • The total mass of reactants = total mass of products
  • The number of each type of atom stays the same
LAW

Mass of reactants = Mass of products

Why Mass is Conserved

During a chemical reaction:

  • Chemical bonds between atoms break
  • Atoms rearrange and form new bonds
  • The same atoms are present before and after - just in different arrangements
  • Since atoms have mass, and no atoms are gained or lost, mass is conserved

Demonstrating Conservation of Mass

To demonstrate conservation of mass experimentally:

  • Carry out the reaction in a closed system (sealed container)
  • Measure the total mass before the reaction
  • Measure the total mass after the reaction
  • The masses should be equal

Example: When copper carbonate is heated in a sealed container, the total mass before and after remains the same.

When Mass Appears to Change

Sometimes the mass appears to change in a reaction. This happens in open systems where gases can escape or enter:

Mass Appears to Decrease

  • When a gas is produced and escapes into the air
  • Example: Heating calcium carbonate produces CO2 gas which escapes
  • CaCO3 → CaO + CO2 (gas escapes)

Mass Appears to Increase

  • When a gas from the air is used in the reaction
  • Example: Burning magnesium combines with oxygen from the air
  • 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO (oxygen adds mass)

Closed vs Open Systems

Closed System Open System
No substances can enter or leave Substances can enter or leave
Mass always stays the same Mass may appear to change
Example: sealed flask Example: open beaker

Key Points to Remember

  • Mass is always conserved in chemical reactions
  • Atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed
  • In closed systems, measured mass stays constant
  • In open systems, mass may appear to change due to gases
  • The total mass of reactants always equals total mass of products
