C3.2.1

Conservation of Mass Calculations

Summary

Using the law of conservation of mass, we can calculate unknown masses in chemical reactions. Since the total mass of reactants equals the total mass of products, we can find missing values by addition or subtraction.

Using Conservation of Mass

FORMULA

Total mass of reactants = Total mass of products

This means:

  • If we know the masses of all reactants and some products, we can calculate the unknown product mass
  • If we know the masses of products and some reactants, we can calculate the unknown reactant mass

Worked Example 1: Finding Product Mass

Question: 10g of calcium carbonate is heated and produces calcium oxide and carbon dioxide. If 5.6g of calcium oxide is produced, what mass of carbon dioxide is formed?

Solution:

  • Mass of reactant (calcium carbonate) = 10g
  • Mass of product 1 (calcium oxide) = 5.6g
  • Mass of product 2 (carbon dioxide) = ?

Using conservation of mass:

Mass of reactants = Mass of products

10g = 5.6g + mass of CO2

Mass of CO2 = 10g - 5.6g = 4.4g

Worked Example 2: Finding Reactant Mass

Question: Magnesium burns in oxygen to form 8g of magnesium oxide. If 4.8g of magnesium was used, what mass of oxygen reacted?

Solution:

  • Mass of reactant 1 (magnesium) = 4.8g
  • Mass of reactant 2 (oxygen) = ?
  • Mass of product (magnesium oxide) = 8g

Using conservation of mass:

Mass of reactants = Mass of products

4.8g + mass of O2 = 8g

Mass of O2 = 8g - 4.8g = 3.2g

Worked Example 3: Multiple Products

Question: 8g of sodium hydroxide reacts with hydrochloric acid. The products are 11.7g of sodium chloride and 3.6g of water. What mass of hydrochloric acid reacted?

Solution:

  • Total mass of products = 11.7g + 3.6g = 15.3g
  • Mass of reactants = Mass of products
  • Mass of NaOH + Mass of HCl = 15.3g
  • 8g + Mass of HCl = 15.3g
  • Mass of HCl = 15.3g - 8g = 7.3g

Steps for Calculations

  1. Write down all the masses you know
  2. Calculate the total mass of reactants OR total mass of products
  3. Use: total mass reactants = total mass products
  4. Rearrange to find the unknown mass
  5. Check your answer makes sense (mass can't be negative)

Key Points to Remember

  • Total mass in = Total mass out
  • Add up all known masses on one side
  • Subtract to find the unknown mass
  • Always include units (usually grams)
  • Check your answer is positive and reasonable
