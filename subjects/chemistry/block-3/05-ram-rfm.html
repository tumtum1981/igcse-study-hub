Relative Atomic Mass and Relative Formula Mass

Summary

The relative atomic mass (Ar) is the mean mass of an atom relative to 1/12th the mass of a carbon-12 atom. The relative formula mass (Mr) is the sum of all the relative atomic masses in a formula.

Relative Atomic Mass (Ar)

  • The relative atomic mass is found on the periodic table
  • It is the mean mass of an atom relative to 1/12th the mass of a carbon-12 atom
  • Symbol: Ar
  • It has no units because it is a ratio

Relative Formula Mass (Mr)

The relative formula mass (also called RFM or Mr) is calculated by adding up the relative atomic masses of all atoms in a formula.

FORMULA

Mr = sum of all Ar values in the formula

Simple Example

Calculate the Mr of NaOH:

  • Na = 23
  • O = 16
  • H = 1
  • Mr = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40

Calculating Mr with Multiple Atoms

When there is more than one atom of an element, multiply the Ar by the number of atoms.

Example 1: H2O

  • H: Ar = 1, there are 2 atoms → 2 × 1 = 2
  • O: Ar = 16, there is 1 atom → 1 × 16 = 16
  • Mr = 2 + 16 = 18

Example 2: CO2

  • C: Ar = 12, there is 1 atom → 1 × 12 = 12
  • O: Ar = 16, there are 2 atoms → 2 × 16 = 32
  • Mr = 12 + 32 = 44

Example 3: C3H8 (propane)

  • C: Ar = 12, there are 3 atoms → 3 × 12 = 36
  • H: Ar = 1, there are 8 atoms → 8 × 1 = 8
  • Mr = 36 + 8 = 44

More Complex Examples

Compound Formula Calculation Mr
Sulfuric acid H2SO4 (2 × 1) + 32 + (4 × 16) 98
Copper sulfate CuSO4 63.5 + 32 + (4 × 16) 159.5
Silver nitrate AgNO3 108 + 14 + (3 × 16) 170
Iron oxide Fe2O3 (2 × 56) + (3 × 16) 160

Conservation of Mass and Mr

In a balanced chemical equation, mass is conserved. This means:

KEY POINT

ΣMr of reactants = ΣMr of products

(The sum of the Mr values on the left equals the sum on the right)

Example: CaCO3 → CaO + CO2

Reactants:

  • CaCO3: 40 + 12 + (3 × 16) = 100

Products:

  • CaO: 40 + 16 = 56
  • CO2: 12 + (2 × 16) = 44
  • Total: 56 + 44 = 100

The total Mr is the same on both sides ✓

Key Points to Remember

  • Ar (relative atomic mass) is found on the periodic table
  • Mr (relative formula mass) = sum of all Ar values in the formula
  • Multiply Ar by the number of atoms of each element
  • Ar and Mr have no units (they are ratios)
  • In balanced equations: ΣMr reactants = ΣMr products

Question 4 [3 marks]

For the reaction: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O, show that the total Mr of reactants equals the total Mr of products.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Reactants: 2H2 = 2 × (2 × 1) = 4; O2 = 2 × 16 = 32; Total = 36 (1 mark)
  • Products: 2H2O = 2 × (2 + 16) = 2 × 18 = 36 (1 mark)
  • Both sides equal 36, so mass is conserved (1 mark)
