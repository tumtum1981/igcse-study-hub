Balancing Symbol Equations

Summary

Symbol equations must be balanced so that there are the same number of each type of atom on both sides. This reflects the law of conservation of mass - atoms cannot be created or destroyed, only rearranged.

Why Balance Equations?

  • Atoms are not created or destroyed in reactions
  • The same atoms must appear on both sides
  • This represents conservation of mass
  • An unbalanced equation is incorrect

Rules for Balancing

  • Only change the big numbers (coefficients) in front of formulae
  • NEVER change the small (subscript) numbers in formulae
  • Count atoms on each side
  • Adjust coefficients until atoms are equal on both sides
IMPORTANT

2H2O means 2 molecules of water (4 H atoms, 2 O atoms)

H4O2 would be a different substance - NEVER write this!

Steps to Balance an Equation

  1. Write the unbalanced equation with correct formulae
  2. Count the atoms of each element on both sides
  3. Start with the most complex molecule or a metal
  4. Add coefficients to balance one element at a time
  5. Check all atoms are balanced
  6. Make sure coefficients are the smallest whole numbers

Worked Example 1

Balance: Mg + O2 → MgO

Step 1: Count atoms

Element Left side Right side
Mg 1 1
O 2 1

Step 2: Balance oxygen by putting 2 in front of MgO

Mg + O2 → 2MgO

Step 3: Now we have 2 Mg on right, so put 2 in front of Mg

Answer: 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO ✓

Worked Example 2

Balance: H2 + O2 → H2O

Count atoms:

  • Left: H = 2, O = 2
  • Right: H = 2, O = 1

Balance oxygen: Put 2 in front of H2O

H2 + O2 → 2H2O

Now: Left O = 2, Right O = 2 ✓

But now: Left H = 2, Right H = 4

Balance hydrogen: Put 2 in front of H2

Answer: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O ✓

Worked Example 3

Balance: Fe + O2 → Fe2O3

This is trickier because O2 has 2 oxygen atoms and Fe2O3 has 3.

Find common multiple of 2 and 3 = 6

Put 3 in front of O2 (gives 6 O atoms)

Put 2 in front of Fe2O3 (needs 6 O atoms) ✓

Now we have 4 Fe on right (2 × 2), so put 4 in front of Fe

Answer: 4Fe + 3O2 → 2Fe2O3 ✓

Common Balanced Equations

Reaction Balanced Equation
Magnesium + Oxygen 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO
Hydrogen + Oxygen 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
Methane combustion CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O
Sodium + Chlorine 2Na + Cl2 → 2NaCl

Key Points to Remember

  • Only change numbers in FRONT of formulae (coefficients)
  • Never change subscript numbers inside formulae
  • Count all atoms on both sides
  • Use smallest whole number coefficients
  • Check your answer by counting atoms again
