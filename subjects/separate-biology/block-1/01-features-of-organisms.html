SB1.1.1

Features of Organisms

Summary

Living organisms are classified into groups based on their shared features. Life on Earth is divided into five kingdoms. Animals are further classified as vertebrates (with a backbone) or invertebrates (without a backbone). Vertebrates are split into five classes, and invertebrates include groups such as arthropods and molluscs.

The Five Kingdoms

We classify life on Earth into five kingdoms:

Kingdom Number of Cells Cell Differences How They Gain Energy Do They Move?
Animals Multicellular (tissues and organs) No cell wall Heterotroph Yes
Plants Multicellular (tissues and organs) Cellulose cell wall + chloroplasts Autotroph No
Fungi Usually multicellular, some unicellular Chitin cell wall Heterotroph Mainly not
Protoctists Mainly unicellular Some have cell wall + chloroplasts Some heterotrophic, some autotrophic Some
Prokaryotes Unicellular Murein cell wall, no nucleus or mitochondria Some heterotrophic, some autotrophic Some

Vertebrates and Invertebrates

Vertebrates are animals that have a spine (backbone) and an internal skeleton. Invertebrates do not have a spine or internal skeleton.

  • Some invertebrates have a hard outer covering called an exoskeleton. As the animal grows, it moults its exoskeleton and a new, larger one hardens.
  • Some invertebrates have a hydrostatic skeleton - a body filled with fluid that supports their body and helps them move.

The Five Vertebrate Classes

Class Reproduction Body Temp Regulated By Obtains Oxygen Through Body Covering
Mammals Give birth to live young Metabolism (warm-blooded) Lungs Fur
Birds Eggs (hard shells, on land) Metabolism (warm-blooded) Lungs Feathers
Reptiles Eggs (rubbery shell, on land) Environment (cold-blooded) Lungs Dry scales
Amphibians Eggs (no shell, in water) Environment (cold-blooded) Skin + lungs Moist, smooth skin
Fish Eggs (no shell, in water) Environment (cold-blooded) Gills Wet scales

Invertebrate Groups

Major invertebrate groups include:

  • Insects: Six legs, body divided into three segments (head, thorax, abdomen), a pair of antennae. Examples: beetles, bees, butterflies, ants
  • Arachnids: Eight legs, body divided into two sections. Examples: spiders, harvestmen, scorpions, ticks
  • Crustaceans: Hard exoskeleton, two pairs of antennae. Examples: crabs, lobsters, shrimp, barnacles
  • Molluscs: Very soft body, hydrostatic skeletons, some have shells. Examples: slugs, snails, squid, octopuses
  • Myriapods: Elongated bodies with many segments, each segment has a pair of jointed legs. Examples: centipedes, millipedes

Arthropods

Arthropods are invertebrates with jointed legs and an exoskeleton. They include four classes:

Class Body Segments Legs Features
Myriapods Elongated (many segments) Lots - each segment has a pair 1 pair of antennae, simple eyes
Insects 3 (head, thorax, abdomen) 3 pairs of jointed legs 1 or 2 pairs of wings, 1 pair of antennae, compound eyes
Arachnids 2 (cephalothorax, abdomen) 4 pairs of legs Piercing jaws, lots of simple eyes
Crustaceans 2 (cephalothorax, abdomen) More than 4 pairs Hard calcium exoskeleton, 2 pairs of antennae, gills, compound eyes

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Heterotroph An organism that obtains its energy by consuming other living things
Autotroph An organism that produces its own food through photosynthesis or chemosynthesis
Exoskeleton A hard outer covering that protects an invertebrate's body
Compound eyes Eyes made up of many individual lenses
Simple eyes Light-sensing organs with a single lens

Key Points to Remember

  • There are five kingdoms: Animals, Plants, Fungi, Protoctists, Prokaryotes
  • Animals are classified as vertebrates (with backbone) or invertebrates (without)
  • Five vertebrate classes: Mammals, Birds, Reptiles, Amphibians, Fish
  • Four arthropod classes: Myriapods, Insects, Arachnids, Crustaceans
  • Mammals and birds are warm-blooded; reptiles, amphibians and fish are cold-blooded

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

State three features that distinguish mammals from reptiles.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Mammals have fur; reptiles have dry scales (1 mark)
  • Mammals give birth to live young; reptiles lay eggs with rubbery shells (1 mark)
  • Mammals are warm-blooded (body temp regulated by metabolism); reptiles are cold-blooded (regulated by environment) (1 mark)
Question 2 [2 marks]

Explain the difference between a vertebrate and an invertebrate.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Vertebrates have a spine/backbone and an internal skeleton (1 mark)
  • Invertebrates do not have a spine or internal skeleton (1 mark)
Question 3 [3 marks]

An animal has 3 pairs of jointed legs, compound eyes, and a body divided into head, thorax and abdomen. State the phylum and class it belongs to, and give one additional feature it might have.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Phylum: invertebrate / arthropod (1 mark)
  • Class: insect (1 mark)
  • Additional feature: 1 or 2 pairs of wings / 1 pair of antennae (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

State two differences between insects and arachnids.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Insects have 3 pairs of legs; arachnids have 4 pairs (1 mark)
  • Insects have 3 body segments; arachnids have 2 (1 mark)
  • Insects have compound eyes; arachnids have simple eyes (1 mark) (any two)
