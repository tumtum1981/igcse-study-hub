Calculating Efficiency

Summary

Efficiency measures how much of the energy put into a device or system is usefully transferred. No device is 100% efficient - some energy is always dissipated (wasted) as thermal energy to the surroundings.

Closed Energy Systems

In a closed energy system, energy can be transferred but mass cannot enter or leave. The total energy in the system stays the same - this is the law of conservation of energy.

Energy can be:

  • Stored - in one of the 8 energy stores
  • Transferred - moved from one store to another
  • Dissipated - spread out to the surroundings (usually as thermal energy)

Energy can never be created or destroyed.

Useful vs Wasted Energy

When energy is transferred by a device:

  • Useful energy - the energy that does the job we want
  • Wasted energy - energy that is not useful (usually thermal energy dissipated to surroundings)

In this example, only 10% of the electrical energy is transferred to light - the rest heats up the bulb and surroundings. This means the bulb has an efficiency of 0.1 (or 10%).

The Efficiency Formula

FORMULA

Efficiency = useful output energy transfer / total input energy transfer

This gives a decimal value between 0 and 1.

To convert to a percentage, multiply by 100:

FORMULA

Efficiency (%) = (useful output / total input) x 100

Key points:

  • Efficiency can never be greater than 1 (or 100%)
  • Higher efficiency = less energy wasted
  • Efficiency has no units (see below)

Worked Examples

Example 1: Wind Turbine

A wind turbine produces 120 MW of electrical energy for every 500 MW of kinetic energy provided by the wind.

CALCULATION

Efficiency = useful output / total input

Efficiency = 120 / 500 = 0.24

Or: 0.24 x 100 = 24% efficient

Example 2: Cycling Up a Hill

Aisha does 12,000 J of work cycling up a hill. At the top, she has transferred 9,000 J into the gravitational store.

CALCULATION

Efficiency = useful output / total input

Efficiency = 9,000 / 12,000 = 0.75

Or: 0.75 x 100 = 75% efficient

Example 3: Lifting Boxes

Lucas lifts a box of books onto a shelf and does 400 J of useful work. In total, he uses 1,000 J of energy.

CALCULATION

Efficiency = useful output / total input

Efficiency = 400 / 1,000 = 0.40

Or: 0.40 x 100 = 40% efficient

Why Efficiency Has No Units

When calculating efficiency, you divide energy by energy:

Joules / Joules = no units

The units cancel out! For example:

400 J / 1000 J = 0.4 (the joules cancel)

This is why efficiency is just a number (a ratio) with no unit. It can also be expressed as a percentage.

Key Points to Remember

  • Efficiency = useful output / total input
  • Multiply by 100 to get percentage efficiency
  • Efficiency is always between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%)
  • Efficiency has no units because joules / joules = dimensionless
  • No device is 100% efficient - some energy is always wasted
  • Wasted energy is usually dissipated as thermal energy
