Resultant Forces

Summary

A resultant force is the single force that has the same effect as all the individual forces acting on an object combined. When multiple forces act on an object, we can find the resultant force using vector addition. The resultant force determines whether an object accelerates, decelerates, or moves at constant velocity.

Calculating Resultant Forces

For forces acting along the same line:

Same direction: add the forces
Resultant = F1 + F2
Opposite directions: subtract the forces
Resultant = F1 - F2 (in direction of larger force)

Balanced and Unbalanced Forces

Balanced Forces Unbalanced Forces
Resultant force = 0 Resultant force is not 0
Object is stationary OR moving at constant velocity Object accelerates (speeds up, slows down, or changes direction)
No change in motion Change in motion

Examples of Resultant Forces

Situation Forces Resultant
Book on table Weight down, Normal force up Zero (balanced)
Falling object (no air resistance) Weight down only Weight (accelerates down)
Car accelerating Driving force > friction Forward (accelerates)
Car at constant speed Driving force = friction Zero (constant velocity)
Car braking Friction > driving force Backward (decelerates)
Terminal velocity Weight = air resistance Zero (constant velocity)

Free Body Diagrams

A free body diagram shows all the forces acting on a single object. Forces are represented by arrows where:

  • The length of the arrow represents the magnitude (size) of the force
  • The direction of the arrow shows the direction of the force
  • Arrows start from the object and point outward

Key Points to Remember

  • Resultant force is the overall combined effect of all forces
  • Balanced forces mean zero resultant and no acceleration
  • Unbalanced forces cause acceleration
  • An object can move at constant velocity with balanced forces
  • Forces in the same direction add together
  • Forces in opposite directions subtract

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

Two forces act on an object: 50 N to the right and 30 N to the left. Calculate the resultant force and state its direction.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Resultant = 50 - 30 = 20 N (1 mark)
  • Direction: to the right (in the direction of the larger force) (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

A car of mass 1200 kg experiences a driving force of 4000 N and a friction force of 1000 N. Calculate: (a) the resultant force, (b) the acceleration of the car.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Resultant force = 4000 - 1000 = 3000 N forward (1 mark)
  • (b) Using F = ma, a = F / m (1 mark)
  • a = 3000 / 1200 = 2.5 m/s2 (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain why a skydiver reaches terminal velocity.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Air resistance increases as speed increases until it equals weight (1 mark)
  • Resultant force becomes zero, so there is no acceleration / constant velocity (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

A box is being pushed with a force of 200 N to the right while friction exerts 200 N to the left. Describe the motion of the box if it: (a) was initially stationary, (b) was initially moving to the right at 5 m/s.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Resultant force = 200 - 200 = 0 N (balanced forces) (1 mark)
  • (a) If stationary, the box will remain stationary (1 mark)
  • (b) If moving at 5 m/s, it will continue at 5 m/s (constant velocity) (1 mark)
