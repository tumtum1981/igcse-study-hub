Density

Summary

Density is a measure of how much mass is contained in a given volume. It tells us how tightly packed the particles are in a substance. Different materials have different densities, which explains why some objects float and others sink.

The Density Formula

density = mass / volume
p = m / V

This can be rearranged to find mass or volume:

mass = density x volume
m = p x V
volume = mass / density
V = m / p

Where:

  • p (rho) = density (measured in kg/m3 or g/cm3)
  • m = mass (measured in kg or g)
  • V = volume (measured in m3 or cm3)

Units of Density

Unit Used For Conversion
kg/m3 SI unit, scientific calculations -
g/cm3 Common for solids and liquids 1 g/cm3 = 1000 kg/m3

Typical Densities

Material Density (kg/m3) Density (g/cm3)
Air 1.2 0.0012
Water 1000 1.0
Ice 920 0.92
Aluminium 2700 2.7
Iron 7800 7.8
Gold 19300 19.3

Floating and Sinking

Whether an object floats or sinks depends on its density compared to the liquid it is in:

Condition Result
Object density < liquid density Object floats
Object density > liquid density Object sinks
Object density = liquid density Object is neutrally buoyant (stays where placed)

Example: Ice floats on water because ice (920 kg/m3) is less dense than water (1000 kg/m3).

Key Points to Remember

  • Density = mass / volume (p = m/V)
  • SI unit is kg/m3
  • Objects less dense than a liquid will float in that liquid
  • Density of water is 1000 kg/m3 or 1 g/cm3
  • Solids are generally denser than liquids, which are denser than gases

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

A block of metal has a mass of 540 g and a volume of 200 cm3. Calculate its density in g/cm3.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Density = mass / volume (1 mark)
  • Density = 540 / 200 = 2.7 g/cm3 (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

A cube of iron has sides of 10 cm. The density of iron is 7800 kg/m3. Calculate the mass of the cube.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Volume = 10 x 10 x 10 = 1000 cm3 = 0.001 m3 (1 mark)
  • Mass = density x volume (1 mark)
  • Mass = 7800 x 0.001 = 7.8 kg (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

A plastic toy has a density of 850 kg/m3. Explain whether it will float or sink in water (density 1000 kg/m3).

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The toy will float (1 mark)
  • Because its density (850 kg/m3) is less than the density of water (1000 kg/m3) (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

A gold bar has a mass of 12.5 kg. If the density of gold is 19,300 kg/m3, calculate the volume of the gold bar in cm3.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Volume = mass / density (1 mark)
  • Volume = 12.5 / 19300 = 0.000648 m3 (1 mark)
  • Volume = 0.000648 x 1,000,000 = 648 cm3 (1 mark)
