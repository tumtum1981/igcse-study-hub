Moments

Summary

A moment is the turning effect of a force. The moment of a force depends on both the size of the force and the distance from the pivot (turning point). Moments are used to understand how levers, seesaws, and many everyday objects work.

The Moment Formula

Moment = Force x Distance
M = F x d

Where:

  • M = moment (measured in Newton-metres, Nm)
  • F = force (measured in Newtons, N)
  • d = perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force (measured in metres, m)

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Moment The turning effect of a force about a pivot
Pivot The fixed point about which rotation occurs
Clockwise moment A moment that causes rotation in the clockwise direction
Anticlockwise moment A moment that causes rotation in the anticlockwise direction

The Principle of Moments

For an object in equilibrium (balanced and not rotating):

Sum of clockwise moments = Sum of anticlockwise moments
The principle of moments

This principle is used to solve problems involving balanced beams, seesaws, and levers.

Increasing Moments

To increase the moment (turning effect), you can:

  • Increase the force applied
  • Increase the distance from the pivot
  • Both of the above

Example: A door handle is placed far from the hinges because a greater distance from the pivot means a larger moment for the same force, making the door easier to open.

Levers

A lever is a simple machine that uses moments to multiply force or distance. Examples include:

  • Crowbar - multiplies force to lift heavy objects
  • Wheelbarrow - reduces effort needed to lift loads
  • Scissors - multiplies force for cutting
  • Seesaw - balances using the principle of moments

Key Points to Remember

  • Moment = Force x Distance (M = Fd)
  • Moments are measured in Newton-metres (Nm)
  • Distance must be perpendicular to the force
  • For balance: clockwise moments = anticlockwise moments
  • Increasing distance from pivot increases the moment

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

A force of 20 N is applied to a spanner at a distance of 0.3 m from the nut. Calculate the moment.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Moment = Force x Distance (1 mark)
  • Moment = 20 x 0.3 = 6 Nm (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

A seesaw is balanced with a child of weight 400 N sitting 2 m from the pivot. How far from the pivot must an adult of weight 800 N sit to balance the seesaw?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Using principle of moments: clockwise moment = anticlockwise moment (1 mark)
  • 400 x 2 = 800 x d (1 mark)
  • d = 800 / 800 = 1 m from the pivot (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain why door handles are placed far from the hinges.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • To increase the distance from the pivot (hinge) (1 mark)
  • This increases the moment / turning effect for the same force, making the door easier to open (1 mark)
Question 4 [4 marks]

A uniform plank of length 4 m and weight 200 N is balanced on a pivot at its centre. A 50 N weight is placed 1.5 m from the pivot on the left side. Calculate the force needed on the right side, placed 1 m from the pivot, to balance the plank.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Weight of plank acts at centre, so creates no moment about the central pivot (1 mark)
  • Anticlockwise moment = 50 x 1.5 = 75 Nm (1 mark)
  • For balance: F x 1 = 75 (1 mark)
  • F = 75 N (1 mark)
