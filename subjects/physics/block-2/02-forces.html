Forces

Summary

A force is a push or pull that can change an object's motion, shape, or direction. Forces are vector quantities, meaning they have both magnitude (size) and direction. Forces are measured in Newtons (N).

Effects of Forces

A force can:

  • Change the speed of an object (speed up or slow down)
  • Change the direction of motion
  • Change the shape of an object (deform it)
  • Start or stop movement

Contact and Non-Contact Forces

Forces can be classified into two main types based on whether objects need to touch:

Contact Forces Non-Contact Forces

Key Definitions

Force Definition
Friction A force that opposes motion between two surfaces in contact
Air resistance A type of friction that opposes motion through air
Tension A pulling force transmitted through a string, rope, or cable
Normal contact force A force perpendicular to a surface that supports an object
Upthrust An upward force exerted by a fluid on an object immersed in it
Weight The gravitational force acting on an object's mass

Friction

Friction is a force that opposes motion. It acts between surfaces that are in contact.

Advantages of Friction Disadvantages of Friction
Allows walking and gripping Causes wear on surfaces
Brakes work due to friction Produces unwanted heat
Writing and drawing need friction Wastes energy
Tyres grip the road Makes movement harder

Reducing friction: Lubrication (oil, grease), streamlining, smooth surfaces, ball bearings.

Increasing friction: Rough surfaces, treads on tyres, grip pads.

Key Points to Remember

  • Forces are measured in Newtons (N)
  • Forces are vector quantities (magnitude and direction)
  • Contact forces require objects to touch
  • Non-contact forces act at a distance
  • Friction always opposes motion
  • Air resistance increases with speed

Question 2 [3 marks]

Explain why friction is both useful and problematic in machines.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Useful: friction allows grip / brakes to work / prevents slipping (1 mark)
  • Problematic: friction causes wear on surfaces / produces heat (1 mark)
  • Friction wastes energy / reduces efficiency of machines (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

State two ways that friction can be reduced in a machine.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Use lubricants such as oil or grease (1 mark)
  • Use smooth surfaces / ball bearings / streamlining (any other valid method) (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

A book is resting on a table. Name and describe the two forces acting on the book and explain why it is not moving.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Weight acts downwards due to gravity (1 mark)
  • Normal contact force / reaction force acts upwards from the table (1 mark)
  • The forces are equal and opposite / balanced, so there is no net force / resultant force is zero (1 mark)
