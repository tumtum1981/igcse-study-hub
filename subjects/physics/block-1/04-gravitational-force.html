Gravitational Force and Weight

Summary

Weight is the force acting on an object due to gravity. It is different from mass, which is the amount of matter in an object. Weight depends on the gravitational field strength (g), which varies depending on where you are in the universe.

The Weight Formula

W = m x g
Weight = mass x gravitational field strength

Where:

  • W = weight (measured in Newtons, N)
  • m = mass (measured in kilograms, kg)
  • g = gravitational field strength (measured in N/kg)

Mass vs Weight

Mass Weight
Amount of matter in an object Force due to gravity on an object
Measured in kilograms (kg) Measured in Newtons (N)
Constant everywhere Changes with location
Scalar quantity Vector quantity (acts downward)
Measured with a balance Measured with a newton meter

Gravitational Field Strength

The value of g varies depending on the planet or moon:

Location g (N/kg)
Earth 10 (or 9.8 for precise calculations)
Moon 1.6
Mars 3.7
Jupiter 25

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Weight The gravitational force acting on an object
Mass The amount of matter in an object
Gravitational field strength The force per unit mass at a point in a gravitational field
Gravitational field The region around a mass where other masses experience a force

Key Points to Remember

  • Weight = mass x gravitational field strength (W = mg)
  • Weight is measured in Newtons (N)
  • Mass is constant; weight changes with location
  • On Earth, g = 10 N/kg (approximately)
  • Weight always acts downward towards the centre of the Earth
  • An object with mass 1 kg has a weight of 10 N on Earth

Question 1 [2 marks]

Calculate the weight of a 75 kg person on Earth (g = 10 N/kg).

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • W = m x g (1 mark)
  • W = 75 x 10 = 750 N (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

An astronaut has a mass of 80 kg. Calculate their weight on: (a) Earth (g = 10 N/kg), (b) the Moon (g = 1.6 N/kg).

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Weight on Earth = 80 x 10 = 800 N (1 mark)
  • (b) Weight on Moon = 80 x 1.6 = 128 N (1 mark)
  • Mass remains 80 kg in both locations (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

An object has a weight of 450 N on Earth. Calculate its mass.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • m = W / g (1 mark)
  • m = 450 / 10 = 45 kg (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

Explain why an astronaut weighs less on the Moon than on Earth, but their mass stays the same.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Mass is the amount of matter, which doesn't change (1 mark)
  • Weight depends on gravitational field strength (1 mark)
  • The Moon has a weaker gravitational field / smaller g value than Earth (1 mark)
