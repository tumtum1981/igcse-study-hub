Work Done and Energy

Summary

Work is done when a force causes an object to move. When work is done, energy is transferred from one form to another. The amount of work done equals the amount of energy transferred. Work and energy are both measured in joules (J).

The Work Done Formula

W = F x d
Work done = Force x Distance moved in direction of force

Where:

  • W = work done (measured in Joules, J)
  • F = force applied (measured in Newtons, N)
  • d = distance moved in the direction of the force (measured in metres, m)

Energy Transfer

When work is done on an object, energy is transferred:

Energy transferred = Work done
Both measured in Joules (J)
Situation Energy Transfer
Lifting an object Chemical energy to gravitational potential energy
Pushing a car Chemical energy to kinetic energy
Friction slowing an object Kinetic energy to thermal energy
Compressing a spring Kinetic energy to elastic potential energy

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Work The product of force and distance moved in the direction of the force
Joule The unit of energy; 1 J = 1 N x 1 m (work done by a 1 N force moving 1 m)
Energy The capacity to do work

Important Points About Work

  • Work is only done if the object moves in the direction of the force
  • No work is done if there is no movement
  • No work is done if the force is perpendicular to the movement
  • Work done against friction is transferred to thermal energy
  • 1 kilojoule (kJ) = 1000 J

Key Points to Remember

  • Work done = Force x Distance (W = Fd)
  • Work done is measured in Joules (J)
  • Energy transferred equals work done
  • The distance must be in the direction of the force
  • No movement = no work done

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

A force of 50 N is used to push a box 8 m across a floor. Calculate the work done.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • W = F x d (1 mark)
  • W = 50 x 8 = 400 J (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

A crane lifts a 200 kg load through a height of 15 m. Calculate: (a) the weight of the load, (b) the work done by the crane. (g = 10 N/kg)

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Weight = m x g = 200 x 10 = 2000 N (1 mark)
  • (b) Work done = F x d = 2000 x 15 (1 mark)
  • Work done = 30,000 J or 30 kJ (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

A student does 600 J of work pushing a trolley. If they applied a force of 40 N, how far did the trolley move?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • d = W / F (1 mark)
  • d = 600 / 40 = 15 m (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

A person pushes against a wall with a force of 100 N for 10 seconds, but the wall does not move. Explain why no work is done, and state the energy transfer.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • No work is done because the wall does not move / distance = 0 (1 mark)
  • Work = F x d = 100 x 0 = 0 J (1 mark)
  • No energy is transferred to the wall (chemical energy in muscles is converted to thermal energy in the person's body) (1 mark)
