Velocity-Time Graphs

Summary

Velocity-time graphs show how the velocity of an object changes over time. Unlike distance-time graphs, the gradient of a velocity-time graph represents acceleration, and the area under the graph represents the distance travelled.

Key Formulas

acceleration = change in velocity / time
a = (v - u) / t
gradient of v-t graph = acceleration
Measured in m/s2
area under v-t graph = distance
Measured in metres (m)

Interpreting Velocity-Time Graphs

Shape of Line What It Means Acceleration
Horizontal line above zero Constant velocity (moving at steady speed) Zero
Horizontal line at zero Object is stationary Zero
Straight line going up Constant acceleration (speeding up) Positive constant
Straight line going down Constant deceleration (slowing down) Negative constant
Curved line Changing acceleration Variable
Line below zero Moving in the opposite direction Depends on gradient

Calculating Distance from Area

The area under a velocity-time graph equals the distance travelled. Use these formulas depending on the shape:

Shape Area Formula
Rectangle Area = base x height = time x velocity
Triangle Area = 1/2 x base x height = 1/2 x time x velocity
Trapezium Area = 1/2 x (a + b) x height

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Acceleration The rate of change of velocity (how quickly velocity changes)
Deceleration Negative acceleration (slowing down)
Uniform acceleration Constant rate of change of velocity

Key Points to Remember

  • Gradient of v-t graph = acceleration
  • Area under v-t graph = distance travelled
  • A horizontal line means constant velocity (not stationary!)
  • Acceleration is measured in m/s2
  • Negative velocity means moving in the opposite direction

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

A car accelerates uniformly from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate: (a) the acceleration, (b) the distance travelled.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration = (v - u) / t = (20 - 0) / 5 = 4 m/s2 (1 mark)
  • (b) Distance = area under graph = 1/2 x base x height (1 mark)
  • Distance = 1/2 x 5 x 20 = 50 m (1 mark)
Question 2 [2 marks]

A velocity-time graph shows a horizontal line at 15 m/s for 8 seconds. What does this tell us about the motion, and what distance was travelled?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The object is moving at constant velocity / no acceleration (1 mark)
  • Distance = velocity x time = 15 x 8 = 120 m (1 mark)
Question 3 [3 marks]

A train travelling at 30 m/s applies its brakes and decelerates uniformly to rest in 15 seconds. Calculate: (a) the deceleration, (b) the braking distance.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Deceleration = (0 - 30) / 15 = -2 m/s2 (or 2 m/s2 deceleration) (1 mark)
  • (b) Distance = area of triangle = 1/2 x 15 x 30 (1 mark)
  • Distance = 225 m (1 mark)
Question 4 [4 marks]

A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 6 seconds, then travels at constant velocity for 10 seconds. Calculate: (a) the acceleration, (b) the total distance travelled.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration = (25 - 10) / 6 = 15/6 = 2.5 m/s2 (1 mark)
  • (b) Distance during acceleration = area of trapezium = 1/2 x (10 + 25) x 6 = 105 m (1 mark)
  • Distance at constant velocity = 25 x 10 = 250 m (1 mark)
  • Total distance = 105 + 250 = 355 m (1 mark)
