Speed, Distance and Time

Summary

Speed is a measure of how fast an object is moving. It tells us the distance an object travels in a given time. Speed is a scalar quantity, meaning it only has magnitude (size) and no direction.

The Speed Formula

The relationship between speed, distance and time is one of the most important equations in physics:

speed = distance / time
v = d / t

This equation can be rearranged to find any of the three variables:

distance = speed x time
d = v x t
time = distance / speed
t = d / v

Key Definitions

Term Definition
Speed The distance travelled per unit time
Average speed Total distance travelled divided by total time taken
Instantaneous speed The speed at a particular moment in time
Velocity Speed in a specified direction (a vector quantity)

Units of Speed

Unit Symbol Used For
Metres per second m/s Scientific calculations (SI unit)
Kilometres per hour km/h Road speeds, everyday use
Miles per hour mph Road speeds (UK/US)

Converting units: To convert km/h to m/s, divide by 3.6. To convert m/s to km/h, multiply by 3.6.

Typical Speeds

Object Speed (m/s) Speed (km/h)
Walking 1.5 5.4
Running 3 10.8
Cycling 6 21.6
Car (urban) 13 48
Sound in air 340 1224

Key Points to Remember

  • Speed = distance / time (v = d/t)
  • The SI unit for speed is metres per second (m/s)
  • Speed is a scalar quantity (magnitude only)
  • Velocity is a vector quantity (magnitude and direction)
  • Average speed uses total distance and total time

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

A car travels 150 km in 2 hours. Calculate the average speed of the car in (a) km/h and (b) m/s.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = distance / time = 150 / 2 = 75 km/h (1 mark)
  • (b) Convert to m/s: 75 / 3.6 = 20.8 m/s (1 mark for method, 1 mark for answer)
Question 2 [2 marks]

A cyclist travels at a constant speed of 8 m/s. How far will they travel in 45 seconds?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Distance = speed x time (1 mark)
  • Distance = 8 x 45 = 360 m (1 mark)
Question 3 [3 marks]

A runner completes a 400 m track in 50 seconds. They then rest for 30 seconds before running another 400 m in 60 seconds. Calculate their average speed for the entire journey.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Total distance = 400 + 400 = 800 m (1 mark)
  • Total time = 50 + 30 + 60 = 140 s (1 mark)
  • Average speed = 800 / 140 = 5.7 m/s (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

Explain the difference between speed and velocity.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Speed is a scalar quantity / has magnitude only (1 mark)
  • Velocity is a vector quantity / has both magnitude and direction (1 mark)
