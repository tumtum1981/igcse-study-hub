Distance-Time Graphs

Summary

Distance-time graphs show how the distance travelled by an object changes over time. The gradient (slope) of a distance-time graph represents the speed of the object. These graphs are essential for understanding and analysing motion.

Key Formula

The gradient of a distance-time graph gives us the speed:

gradient = change in distance / change in time
gradient = speed (m/s)

Interpreting Distance-Time Graphs

Shape of Line What It Means Speed
Horizontal line Object is stationary (not moving) Zero
Straight diagonal line (upward) Object moving at constant speed Constant
Steep diagonal line Object moving fast High
Gentle diagonal line Object moving slowly Low
Curved line (getting steeper) Object accelerating Increasing
Curved line (getting less steep) Object decelerating Decreasing

Calculating Speed from Graphs

To calculate speed from a distance-time graph:

  1. Choose two points on the line
  2. Calculate the change in distance (rise)
  3. Calculate the change in time (run)
  4. Divide: speed = rise / run
speed = (d2 - d1) / (t2 - t1)
Where d = distance, t = time

Key Points to Remember

  • Gradient of a distance-time graph = speed
  • A horizontal line means the object is stationary
  • A steeper line means a faster speed
  • A curved line indicates changing speed (acceleration or deceleration)
  • Distance-time graphs cannot show direction of travel

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Do not confuse distance-time graphs with velocity-time graphs
  • Remember: the gradient gives speed, not acceleration
  • Distance can never decrease on a distance-time graph (it shows total distance travelled)
  • A negative gradient would mean moving backwards - use displacement-time graphs for this

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [2 marks]

On a distance-time graph, a car travels 200 m in 10 seconds. Calculate the speed of the car.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Speed = gradient = distance / time (1 mark)
  • Speed = 200 / 10 = 20 m/s (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

A distance-time graph shows a horizontal line at 50 m for 20 seconds, followed by a straight line reaching 150 m at 40 seconds. Describe the motion and calculate the speed during the second part of the journey.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • First part: Object is stationary for 20 seconds (1 mark)
  • Second part: Object moves at constant speed (1 mark)
  • Speed = (150 - 50) / (40 - 20) = 100 / 20 = 5 m/s (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain what a curved line getting steeper on a distance-time graph indicates about the object's motion.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The object is accelerating / speeding up (1 mark)
  • The gradient is increasing, so speed is increasing (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

Two objects A and B are shown on the same distance-time graph. Object A's line has a gradient of 5 m/s. Object B's line has a gradient of 8 m/s. After 10 seconds, how much further will B have travelled than A?

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Distance A = speed x time = 5 x 10 = 50 m (1 mark)
  • Distance B = speed x time = 8 x 10 = 80 m (1 mark)
  • Difference = 80 - 50 = 30 m (1 mark)
