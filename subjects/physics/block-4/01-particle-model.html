The Particle Model

Three States of Matter

All substances are made of matter. Substances commonly exist in one of three states of matter: solid, liquid, or gas.

The particle model is used to represent matter in different states. It uses small solid spheres to represent particles.

Particles in Each State

Property Solid Liquid Gas
Arrangement Regular Random Random
Spacing Touching, no spaces Touching, no spaces Far apart, spaces between
Movement Vibrate in fixed position Move past each other Move quickly in all directions
Forces of attraction Cannot overcome them Partially overcome Completely overcome

In solids and liquids, particles are held together by forces of attraction. These forces act in all directions and vary in strength depending on the substance and its state. As particles gain energy, they are able to overcome these forces.

Properties of States of Matter

Shape

State Takes shape of container? Why?
Solid No Particles held in fixed position by forces of attraction
Liquid Yes - the bottom of it Particles free to move past each other
Gas Yes - the whole of it Particles move quickly in all directions

Compression

State Can be compressed? Why?
Solid No Particles already touching - cannot be forced closer together
Liquid No Particles already touching - cannot be forced closer together
Gas Yes Particles have space between them - can be forced closer together

* IGCSE Practice Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

Describe the arrangement and movement of particles in a liquid.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Particles are randomly arranged (1 mark)
  • Particles are close together / touching (1 mark)
  • Particles can move past neighbouring particles (1 mark)
Question 2 [2 marks]

Explain why a gas can be compressed but a solid cannot.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • In a gas, particles have space between them so can be forced closer together (1 mark)
  • In a solid, particles are already touching so cannot be forced closer (1 mark)
Question 3 [2 marks]

Explain why a liquid takes the shape of the bottom of its container but a gas fills the whole container.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Liquid particles can move past each other but are still held close by forces of attraction (1 mark)
  • Gas particles have enough energy to completely overcome forces of attraction and move quickly in all directions (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

State two differences between the particles in a solid and the particles in a gas.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Any two from: solid particles are in a regular arrangement / gas particles are random; solid particles vibrate in fixed position / gas particles move quickly in all directions; solid particles are touching / gas particles have spaces between them (1 mark each)
Question 5 [3 marks]

If you take the lid off a container of gas, the gas will fill the room. If you take the lid off a container of solid, it stays in the container. Explain why, using the words 'particles', 'forces of attraction' and 'energy'.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Gas particles have enough energy to overcome the forces of attraction between them (1 mark)
  • They are free to move quickly in all directions and can escape the container (1 mark)
  • Solid particles do not have enough energy to overcome forces of attraction, so they stay in their fixed positions (1 mark)
