Question 1

A student wants to confirm that a food sample contains lipids. She shakes the sample with ethanol, then pours the ethanol into cold water. No white emulsion appears. What is the most likely explanation?

A The ethanol was too concentrated
B The water was too warm, dissolving the lipids
C The food sample does not contain lipids
D The lipids were denatured by the ethanol
Question 2

A Kills bacteria in food and provides the optimum pH for pepsin
B Emulsifies fats and neutralises bile
C Breaks down starch and kills bacteria
D Absorbs nutrients and provides the optimum pH for amylase
Question 3

A Both experiments will produce the same amount of product because the enzyme is the same
B Experiment A will produce more product because lower temperatures protect enzymes
C Experiment B will produce less product because 37 degrees C denatures the enzyme
D Experiment B will produce more product because 37 degrees C is closer to the optimum for most human enzymes

A Stomach, assisted by hydrochloric acid
B Small intestine, assisted by bile which emulsifies fats
C Mouth, assisted by saliva
D Large intestine, assisted by bacteria
Question 5

B Calcium deficiency - treated by drinking milk
C Vitamin D deficiency - treated by eating oily fish
D Vitamin C deficiency (scurvy) - treated by eating citrus fruits
Question 6

A Biuret reagent detects protein (present in egg white) but not sugars
B Biuret reagent detects starch in egg white but glucose is too dilute
C Egg white contains lipids which react with Biuret reagent
D The glucose in tube 2 should have turned purple if heated

A Absorption - chemical digestion
B Peristalsis - mechanical digestion
C Peristalsis - chemical digestion
D Emulsification - mechanical digestion
Question 8

A The enzyme needs time to reactivate after cooling
B The substrate was also denatured by the cooling process
C Denaturation is permanent - the active site shape cannot be restored by lowering the temperature
D The enzyme evaporated at 80 degrees C and was no longer present
Question 9

Which statement correctly explains how bile helps lipase to work more efficiently?

A Bile breaks large fat droplets into smaller droplets, increasing the surface area for lipase to act on
B Bile chemically digests lipids into fatty acids before lipase finishes the process
C Bile changes the shape of lipase's active site so it can fit more substrates
D Bile increases the temperature of the small intestine to optimum levels
Question 10

A The athlete needs less protein because their muscles are already developed
B The elderly person needs more carbohydrates to maintain body temperature
C The athlete needs more energy from carbohydrates for exercise and more protein for muscle growth and repair
D Both people need exactly the same diet because they are the same species
Question 11

A Each lipid molecule contains one glycerol bonded to one fatty acid
B Each lipid molecule contains one glycerol bonded to three fatty acids
C Each lipid molecule contains three glycerols bonded to one fatty acid

A The person would vomit more frequently
B The person would become unable to digest protein
C The person would produce very hard, dry faeces

Question 13

A The active site shape is unchanged; molecules simply have too little kinetic energy for frequent collisions
B The enzyme is frozen solid and its bonds have broken
C The active site has changed shape but will recover when warmed
D The substrate is too cold to enter the active site

A Breaking down amino acids in the liver to form urea
B Respiring amino acids to release energy
