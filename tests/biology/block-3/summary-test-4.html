20 Multiple Choice Questions | All Block 3 Topics

Question 1

A student adds ethanol to a food sample, then pours the mixture into water. A white cloudy emulsion forms. She then adds Biuret reagent to a second sample and observes a purple colour. What does the food contain?

A Lipids and starch
B Lipids and protein
C Reducing sugars and protein
D Starch and lipids
Question 2

A Bile is produced in the liver, not the pancreas
B Bile works in the small intestine rather than the stomach
C Bile breaks down food into smaller molecules
D Bile physically emulsifies fat rather than chemically breaking bonds
Question 3

B The active site changes shape to fit more substrates
C More enzyme molecules are being produced at higher temperatures

A The temperature has increased above the optimum
B The lipase has been denatured by the milk
C Fatty acids produced by lipid digestion have lowered the pH
D The protein in milk has been broken down into amino acids

D Calcium - needed for nerve impulses only

B The solution turns green after heating

Question 7

B Capillaries increase surface area; thin walls secrete mucus

Question 8

A Pepsin would work faster at pH 8 because the pH is higher
B At pH 8, pepsin would be denatured as this is far from its optimum pH
C Pepsin and trypsin are both proteases so they always work at the same rate
D Trypsin cannot function at pH 8 either

B The amylase has been denatured and stopped working
C There is a large amount of starch still present
D The iodine reagent has expired and is no longer working
