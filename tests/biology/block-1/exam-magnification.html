Exam: Magnification

Exam-Style Questions | Microscopy and Calculations

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

A student draws a cell that appears 45 mm wide in their drawing. The actual cell is 0.09 mm wide. Calculate the magnification of the drawing. Show your working.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Magnification = Image size / Actual size (1 mark)
  • Magnification = 45 / 0.09 (1 mark)
  • Magnification = x500 (1 mark)
Question 2 [4 marks]

A microscope image shows a cell that is 40 mm long. The magnification used was x800. Calculate the actual size of the cell in micrometres (um). Show your working.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Actual size = Image size / Magnification (1 mark)
  • Actual size = 40 / 800 (1 mark)
  • Actual size = 0.05 mm (1 mark)
  • Convert to um: 0.05 x 1000 = 50 um (1 mark)
Question 3 [3 marks]

Compare light microscopes and electron microscopes. State one similarity and two differences between them.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Similarity:

  • Both are used to view specimens that are too small to see with the naked eye / both magnify images (1 mark)

Differences:

  • Electron microscopes have higher magnification (up to x2,000,000) than light microscopes (up to x1500) (1 mark)
  • Light microscopes can view living specimens, electron microscopes cannot (1 mark)
  • Electron microscopes have higher resolution / can see more detail (alternative)
Question 4 [2 marks]

A light microscope has an eyepiece lens of x10 and three objective lenses: x4, x10, and x40. Calculate the maximum total magnification possible with this microscope.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Total magnification = Eyepiece x Objective (1 mark)
  • Maximum = 10 x 40 = x400 (1 mark)
Question 5 [3 marks]

A cell has an actual diameter of 25 um. A student wants to draw this cell with a magnification of x400. Calculate the diameter of the drawing in mm. Show your working.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Image size = Actual size x Magnification (1 mark)
  • Image size = 25 x 400 = 10,000 um (1 mark)
  • Convert to mm: 10,000 / 1000 = 10 mm (1 mark)
Question 6 [3 marks]

Explain why electron microscopes allow scientists to see more detail in cells than light microscopes.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Electron microscopes use a beam of electrons instead of light (1 mark)
  • Electrons have a much shorter wavelength than light (1 mark)
  • This gives electron microscopes higher resolution / ability to distinguish between two points close together (1 mark)
Question 7 [4 marks]

A student measures a bacterial cell in a micrograph. The image of the cell is 15 mm long, and the scale bar on the micrograph shows that 5 mm represents 2 um. Calculate the actual length of the bacterial cell in micrometres. Show your working.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Scale: 5 mm = 2 um, so 1 mm = 0.4 um (1 mark)
  • Or: magnification = 5 / 0.002 = x2500 (1 mark)
  • Actual size = 15 mm / 2500 = 0.006 mm (1 mark)
  • Convert: 0.006 x 1000 = 6 um (1 mark)
  • Or: 15 mm x 0.4 um/mm = 6 um
Question 8 [2 marks]

State why magnification has no units.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Magnification is a ratio (1 mark)
  • It is calculated by dividing one length by another, so the units cancel out (1 mark)
