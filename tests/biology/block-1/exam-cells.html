Exam: Cells

Exam-Style Questions | Cell Structure and Organelles

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

The diagram shows an animal cell. Name structures A, B and C, and state the function of each.

A - small rod-shaped structure
B - large circular structure
C - outer boundary of the cell

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • A: Mitochondria - site of aerobic respiration / releases energy from glucose (1 mark)
  • B: Nucleus - controls cell activities / contains genetic material (DNA) (1 mark)
  • C: Cell membrane - controls what enters and leaves the cell (1 mark)
Question 2 [4 marks]

Compare the structure of plant cells and animal cells. State two similarities and two differences between them.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Similarities (any 2):

  • Both have a nucleus (1 mark)
  • Both have a cell membrane (1 mark)
  • Both have cytoplasm / mitochondria / ribosomes (alternative)

Differences (any 2):

  • Plant cells have a cell wall, animal cells do not (1 mark)
  • Plant cells have chloroplasts, animal cells do not (1 mark)
  • Plant cells have a large permanent vacuole, animal cells have small or no vacuole (alternative)
Question 3 [3 marks]

Explain why muscle cells contain a large number of mitochondria.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Muscle cells need to contract (1 mark)
  • Contraction requires a lot of energy (1 mark)
  • Mitochondria carry out aerobic respiration to release energy (1 mark)
Question 4 [4 marks]

A student observes a cell under a microscope. The cell has a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large vacuole. Identify what type of cell this is, and explain what each of these three structures does.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • This is a plant cell (1 mark)
  • Cell wall - provides support and prevents the cell from bursting (1 mark)
  • Chloroplasts - contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis / absorb light energy (1 mark)
  • Large vacuole - contains cell sap and keeps the cell turgid (firm) (1 mark)
Question 5 [3 marks]

Explain why plant cells do not burst when placed in pure water, but animal cells can burst under the same conditions.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Water enters both types of cell by osmosis (1 mark)
  • Plant cells have a cell wall made of cellulose (1 mark)
  • The cell wall is strong/rigid and prevents the cell from bursting / animal cells have no cell wall so nothing prevents them bursting (1 mark)
Question 6 [2 marks]

State the function of ribosomes and describe where they are found in the cell.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Function: Site of protein synthesis / make proteins (1 mark)
  • Location: Found in the cytoplasm / attached to endoplasmic reticulum (1 mark)
Question 7 [4 marks]

A root cell from a plant does not contain chloroplasts, but a leaf cell does. Explain why this difference exists and what it tells us about where photosynthesis occurs.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Chloroplasts are needed for photosynthesis (1 mark)
  • Photosynthesis requires light (1 mark)
  • Root cells are underground where there is no light (1 mark)
  • Leaf cells are exposed to light so can carry out photosynthesis (1 mark)
