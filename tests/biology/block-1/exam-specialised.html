Exam: Specialised Cells

Exam-Style Questions | Cell Adaptations and Differentiation

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [4 marks]

Red blood cells are specialised for carrying oxygen around the body. Describe four adaptations of red blood cells and explain how each adaptation helps them carry out their function.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • No nucleus - provides more space for haemoglobin to carry oxygen (1 mark)
  • Biconcave disc shape - increases surface area for faster oxygen exchange (1 mark)
  • Contains haemoglobin - binds to oxygen for transport (1 mark)
  • Flexible membrane - allows them to squeeze through narrow capillaries (1 mark)
Question 2 [3 marks]

Sperm cells are adapted for their function of fertilising an egg. Describe three adaptations of a sperm cell and explain how each helps with fertilisation.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Tail (flagellum) - allows the sperm to swim towards the egg (1 mark)
  • Many mitochondria - release energy for swimming (1 mark)
  • Acrosome in the head - contains enzymes to penetrate/digest the egg membrane (1 mark)
  • Streamlined shape - reduces resistance when swimming (alternative)
Question 3 [4 marks]

Root hair cells are found in plant roots. Describe how root hair cells are adapted for absorbing water and mineral ions from the soil.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Long hair-like projection - increases surface area for absorption (1 mark)
  • Thin cell wall - shortens the diffusion distance (1 mark)
  • Large vacuole - maintains water potential gradient for osmosis (1 mark)
  • Many mitochondria - provide energy for active transport of mineral ions (1 mark)
Question 4 [2 marks]

Define the term 'differentiation' and explain its importance in multicellular organisms.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Differentiation is the process by which cells become specialised for a particular function (1 mark)
  • It allows organisms to have cells that are efficient at specific tasks / allows division of labour between cells (1 mark)
Question 5 [4 marks]

Nerve cells (neurones) are specialised to transmit electrical impulses. Describe four adaptations of nerve cells and explain how each helps them carry out their function.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Long axon - allows impulses to be transmitted over long distances (1 mark)
  • Branched endings (dendrites) - can connect to many other neurones (1 mark)
  • Myelin sheath - insulates the axon and speeds up impulse transmission (1 mark)
  • Many mitochondria - provide energy for impulse transmission (1 mark)
Question 6 [3 marks]

Palisade mesophyll cells are found near the top surface of leaves. Explain how three features of these cells make them efficient at photosynthesis.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Many chloroplasts - maximise the rate of photosynthesis (1 mark)
  • Located near top of leaf - close to light source for maximum light absorption (1 mark)
  • Tall, column-shaped - many cells can be packed tightly together (1 mark)
  • Thin cell walls - allow easy gas exchange (alternative)
Question 7 [3 marks]

A student states that "the structure of a specialised cell is always related to its function." Using one named example, explain what this statement means.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Example: Red blood cell (or any other valid example)

  • Function: To carry oxygen around the body (1 mark)
  • Structure: Has no nucleus (1 mark)
  • Relationship: The lack of nucleus provides more space for haemoglobin to carry more oxygen (1 mark)
