Plant Classification - Exam Questions

Exam-style questions on plant groups, monocots vs dicots, and flowering plants

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [4 marks]

State four differences between monocotyledons and dicotyledons.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Monocots have one cotyledon / seed leaf; dicots have two cotyledons (1 mark)
  • Monocots have parallel leaf veins; dicots have branching / net-like leaf veins (1 mark)
  • Monocot flower parts are in multiples of 3; dicot flower parts are in multiples of 4 or 5 (1 mark)
  • Monocots have scattered vascular bundles in stem; dicots have vascular bundles arranged in a ring (1 mark)
  • Monocots have fibrous roots; dicots have a taproot system (1 mark) (any four)
Question 2 [4 marks]

A student examines a flowering plant and records the following observations:

  • The leaves have parallel veins running along their length
  • The flower has 6 petals
  • The roots are thin and spread out in many directions

(a) Is this plant a monocotyledon or a dicotyledon? [1 mark]

(b) Explain your answer using evidence from the observations. [3 marks]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Monocotyledon / monocot (1 mark)
  • (b) Parallel leaf veins are a monocot feature (1 mark)
  • (b) 6 petals is a multiple of 3, which is characteristic of monocots (1 mark)
  • (b) Fibrous / spreading root system is typical of monocots (1 mark)
Question 3 [5 marks]

Compare mosses and ferns in terms of their structure and reproduction.

Feature Mosses Ferns
Vascular tissue ..................... .....................
True roots ..................... .....................
Reproduction ..................... .....................
* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Vascular tissue: Mosses - absent / no vascular tissue (1 mark); Ferns - present / have vascular tissue (1 mark)
  • True roots: Mosses - absent / no true roots (1 mark); Ferns - present / have true roots (1 mark)
  • Reproduction: Both reproduce by spores (1 mark)
Question 4 [3 marks]

Explain why mosses are usually found in damp, shaded habitats.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Mosses have no vascular tissue, so they cannot transport water efficiently through the plant (1 mark)
  • They need to absorb water directly through their surface, so they must stay moist (1 mark)
  • Their male gametes need water to swim to the female gametes for reproduction (1 mark)
Question 5 [4 marks]

Describe how flowering plants (angiosperms) differ from conifers (gymnosperms) in their method of reproduction.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Flowering plants produce flowers; conifers do not produce flowers (1 mark)
  • Flowering plants produce seeds enclosed within fruits; conifers produce naked seeds in cones (1 mark)
  • Flowering plants are pollinated by insects, wind, or other animals; conifers are mainly wind-pollinated (1 mark)
  • Flowering plant seeds are often dispersed by animals eating fruits; conifer seeds are dispersed by wind (1 mark)
Question 6 [5 marks]

The table shows features of four plant groups.

Plant Group Vascular Tissue Seeds Flowers
A Yes Yes, in fruit Yes
B Yes Yes, in cones No
C Yes No, spores No
D No No, spores No

(a) Identify each plant group (A, B, C, D). [4 marks]

(b) Give one example of a plant from group B. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) A = Flowering plants / Angiosperms (1 mark)
  • (a) B = Conifers / Gymnosperms (1 mark)
  • (a) C = Ferns / Pteridophytes (1 mark)
  • (a) D = Mosses / Bryophytes (1 mark)
  • (b) Pine / spruce / fir / cedar / any named conifer (1 mark)
Question 7 [4 marks]

A gardener wants to determine if an unknown seedling is a monocot or dicot. Describe two methods they could use to identify the plant, and explain what they would look for.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Method 1:

  • Examine the leaf vein pattern (1 mark)
  • Parallel veins indicate a monocot; branching / net-like veins indicate a dicot (1 mark)

Method 2:

  • Count the number of seed leaves / cotyledons on the seedling (1 mark)
  • One cotyledon indicates a monocot; two cotyledons indicate a dicot (1 mark)

Other acceptable methods:

  • Wait for flowers and count petals (multiples of 3 = monocot; 4 or 5 = dicot)
  • Cut stem cross-section and look at vascular bundle arrangement (scattered = monocot; ring = dicot)
  • Examine root system (fibrous = monocot; taproot = dicot)
Question 8 [6 marks]

The diagram below shows a simplified classification of the plant kingdom.

                    PLANTS
                       |
        +--------------+---------------+
        |                              |
   Non-vascular                    Vascular
    (Mosses)                          |
                        +-------------+-------------+
                        |                           |
                  Non-seed                     Seed-bearing
                   (Ferns)                          |
                                    +---------------+---------------+
                                    |                               |
                              Gymnosperms                    Angiosperms
                              (Conifers)                   (Flowering plants)
                                                                   |
                                                    +--------------+-------------+
                                                    |                            |
                                               Monocots                      Dicots

(a) Using the diagram, explain the key feature that distinguishes mosses from all other plant groups. [2 marks]

(b) Explain the key difference between gymnosperms and angiosperms. [2 marks]

(c) Give one example each of a monocot and a dicot. [2 marks]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Mosses are non-vascular / lack vascular tissue (1 mark)
  • (a) All other plant groups have vascular tissue for transporting water and nutrients (1 mark)
  • (b) Angiosperms produce flowers and seeds enclosed in fruits (1 mark)
  • (b) Gymnosperms do not produce flowers and have naked seeds / seeds in cones (1 mark)
  • (c) Monocot example: grass / wheat / rice / maize / lily / orchid / bamboo (1 mark)
  • (c) Dicot example: rose / oak / bean / sunflower / apple / tomato (1 mark)
