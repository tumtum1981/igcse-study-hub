Evolutionary Trees - Exam Questions

Exam-style questions on phylogenetic trees, common ancestors, and evolutionary evidence

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

(a) Define the term 'common ancestor'. [2 marks]

(b) What is the term used for a branching point on an evolutionary tree? [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) An ancestral species / organism (1 mark)
  • (a) From which two or more different species have evolved (1 mark)
  • (b) Node (1 mark)
Question 2 [4 marks]

The diagram below represents a simplified evolutionary tree for four species (P, Q, R, and S).

        P       Q       R       S
        |       |       |       |
        +---+---+       +---+---+
            |               |
            +-------+-------+
                    |
                  Root

(a) Which two species are most closely related? Explain your answer. [2 marks]

(b) Which species shares the oldest common ancestor with species P? [1 mark]

(c) What does the root of the tree represent? [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) P and Q are most closely related (accept R and S) (1 mark)
  • (a) Because they share the most recent common ancestor / their branches join closest to the tips (1 mark)
  • (b) Species S (or R) - any species on the opposite main branch (1 mark)
  • (c) The oldest common ancestor of all organisms on the tree (1 mark)
Question 3 [4 marks]

State four types of evidence that scientists can use to construct evolutionary trees.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • DNA / gene sequences (1 mark)
  • Physical features / morphology / anatomy / body structure (1 mark)
  • Protein / amino acid sequences (1 mark)
  • Fossil record / evidence (1 mark)
  • Embryo / embryonic development patterns (1 mark) (any four)
Question 4 [4 marks]

Explain why DNA sequence comparison is considered one of the most reliable methods for determining evolutionary relationships between species.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • DNA is inherited from ancestors / passed from generation to generation (1 mark)
  • Closely related species have more similar DNA sequences (1 mark)
  • DNA provides precise / quantifiable data that can be measured and compared accurately (1 mark)
  • DNA mutations accumulate over time at a relatively constant rate / acts as a molecular clock (1 mark)
  • DNA comparison is not affected by superficial similarities caused by similar environments / convergent evolution (1 mark) (any four)
Question 5 [5 marks]

Scientists compared the DNA of five primate species. The table shows the percentage of DNA that differs from human DNA.

Species % DNA difference from humans
Chimpanzee 1.2%
Gorilla 1.6%
Orangutan 3.1%
Gibbon 5.0%
Rhesus monkey 7.0%

(a) Which species is most closely related to humans? Explain your answer. [2 marks]

(b) Using this data, place these species in order from most closely related to humans to least closely related. [2 marks]

(c) Suggest why the DNA comparison method might be more reliable than comparing physical features alone. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Chimpanzee (1 mark)
  • (a) Because it has the smallest percentage difference in DNA / shares the most DNA with humans (1 mark)
  • (b) Chimpanzee > Gorilla > Orangutan > Gibbon > Rhesus monkey (2 marks for correct order; 1 mark for mostly correct)
  • (c) Physical features can be similar due to similar environments / convergent evolution rather than shared ancestry / DNA gives more precise quantitative data (1 mark)
Question 6 [3 marks]

Explain how an evolutionary tree shows that species X and species Y share a more recent common ancestor than species X and species Z.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The node / branching point where X and Y meet is closer to the tips of the tree (1 mark)
  • The node where X and Z meet is closer to the root / base of the tree (1 mark)
  • Branches that join closer to the tips represent more recent divergence / closer relationships (1 mark)
Question 7 [6 marks]

Scientists studied the amino acid sequences in the haemoglobin protein of different vertebrates. They found the following number of amino acid differences compared to human haemoglobin:

Species Number of amino acid differences
Chimpanzee 0
Dog 15
Chicken 45
Frog 67

(a) What does the data suggest about the evolutionary relationship between humans and chimpanzees? [2 marks]

(b) Draw a simple evolutionary tree showing the relationships between humans, dogs, chickens, and frogs based on this data. [3 marks]

(c) Explain why scientists use protein sequences as evidence for evolutionary relationships. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Humans and chimpanzees are very closely related / share a recent common ancestor (1 mark)
  • (a) They have identical haemoglobin / no amino acid differences (1 mark)
  • (b) Tree should show:
    • Humans and chimpanzees on branches very close together / same node (1 mark)
    • Dog branching off before human/chimp split (1 mark)
    • Chicken and frog on more distant branches, with frog being the most distant (1 mark)
  • (c) Proteins are coded for by DNA / similar proteins indicate similar DNA / inherited from common ancestors (1 mark)
Question 8 [3 marks]

Explain why an evolutionary tree can be redrawn with branches in different positions without changing its meaning.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Branches can be rotated around nodes / branching points (1 mark)
  • The important information is which species share nodes / common ancestors, not the left-right order (1 mark)
  • The evolutionary relationships shown remain the same regardless of how the branches are arranged (1 mark)
