Species and Binomial Naming - Exam Questions

Exam-style questions on species definition, binomial nomenclature, and classification hierarchy

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [3 marks]

Define the term 'species' and explain why the ability to produce fertile offspring is an important part of this definition.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • A species is a group of organisms that can interbreed (1 mark)
  • To produce fertile offspring (1 mark)
  • This is important because it ensures the species can continue / maintain a breeding population / distinguishes from hybrids which are infertile (1 mark)
Question 2 [4 marks]

The table shows the scientific names of four big cats:

Common Name Scientific Name
Lion Panthera leo
Tiger Panthera tigris
Leopard Panthera pardus
Cheetah Acinonyx jubatus

(a) Identify the genus name for the lion. [1 mark]

(b) Explain what the scientific names tell us about the relationship between the lion, tiger, and leopard. [2 marks]

(c) What does the cheetah's scientific name suggest about its relationship to the other three cats? [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Panthera (1 mark)
  • (b) They share the same genus name (Panthera) (1 mark)
  • (b) This shows they are closely related / share a recent common ancestor (1 mark)
  • (c) The cheetah belongs to a different genus (Acinonyx), so it is less closely related to the other three cats (1 mark)
Question 3 [4 marks]

A liger is the offspring of a male lion (Panthera leo) and a female tiger (Panthera tigris). Ligers are usually infertile.

(a) What term is used to describe offspring like the liger? [1 mark]

(b) Explain why the existence of the liger is evidence that lions and tigers are different species. [3 marks]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Hybrid (1 mark)
  • (b) Although lions and tigers can interbreed / mate (1 mark)
  • (b) The offspring (liger) is infertile / cannot reproduce (1 mark)
  • (b) Members of the same species must produce fertile offspring, so lions and tigers must be different species (1 mark)
Question 4 [4 marks]

State four advantages of using the binomial naming system rather than common names for organisms.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Universal - the same scientific name is used worldwide (1 mark)
  • Unique - each species has only one scientific name (1 mark)
  • Shows relationships - organisms in the same genus share the first part of their name (1 mark)
  • Precise - avoids confusion from different common names in different regions (1 mark)
  • Stable - scientific names rarely change, unlike common names (1 mark)
  • Avoids language barriers - works across different languages (1 mark) (any four)
Question 5 [5 marks]

The classification hierarchy for humans is shown below:

Kingdom: Animalia > Phylum: Chordata > Class: Mammalia > Order: Primates > Family: Hominidae > Genus: Homo > Species: sapiens

(a) State the correct way to write the full scientific name for humans. [2 marks]

(b) A gorilla belongs to the same family (Hominidae) as humans but has the scientific name Gorilla gorilla. What does this tell us about the relationship between humans and gorillas? [2 marks]

(c) Name the classification level that contains closely related genera. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Homo sapiens - with Homo capitalised and sapiens lowercase (1 mark)
  • (a) Written in italics (or underlined if handwritten) (1 mark)
  • (b) Humans and gorillas are closely related / share a common ancestor (1 mark)
  • (b) They belong to different genera, so they are not as closely related as organisms in the same genus (1 mark)
  • (c) Family (1 mark)
Question 6 [3 marks]

A student writes the scientific name for a domestic dog as "canis Familiaris". Identify and correct the errors in how this name has been written.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • The genus name (Canis) should start with a capital letter, not lowercase (1 mark)
  • The species name (familiaris) should start with a lowercase letter, not capital (1 mark)
  • The name should be written in italics: Canis familiaris (or underlined if handwritten) (1 mark)
Question 7 [6 marks]

The grey wolf (Canis lupus) and the domestic dog (Canis familiaris) can interbreed and produce fertile offspring. The red fox has the scientific name Vulpes vulpes.

(a) Based on the species definition, discuss whether the grey wolf and domestic dog could be considered the same species. [3 marks]

(b) What does the scientific name of the red fox tell us about its relationship to wolves and dogs? [2 marks]

(c) Suggest why scientists might still classify wolves and dogs as separate species despite being able to produce fertile offspring. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) According to the species definition, organisms that can interbreed to produce fertile offspring are the same species (1 mark)
  • (a) Wolves and dogs can produce fertile offspring (1 mark)
  • (a) This suggests they could be considered the same species / some scientists classify the dog as a subspecies of wolf (1 mark)
  • (b) The red fox belongs to a different genus (Vulpes) (1 mark)
  • (b) This means it is less closely related to wolves and dogs than they are to each other (1 mark)
  • (c) They have different physical features / behaviours / natural habitats / have been separated for thousands of years through domestication (1 mark)
Question 8 [3 marks]

Put the following classification levels in order from largest (most organisms) to smallest (fewest organisms): Class, Family, Genus, Kingdom, Order, Phylum, Species.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Correct order:

  1. Kingdom
  2. Phylum
  3. Class
  4. Order
  5. Family
  6. Genus
  7. Species

(3 marks for completely correct order; 2 marks for 5-6 in correct positions; 1 mark for 3-4 in correct positions)
