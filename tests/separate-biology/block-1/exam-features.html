Features of Organisms - Exam Questions

Exam-style questions on the five kingdoms, classification, and features of organisms

* Exam-Style Questions

Question 1 [4 marks]

Complete the table below by stating two key features for each kingdom.

Kingdom Feature 1 Feature 2
Fungi ..................... .....................
Prokaryotes ..................... .....................
* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

Fungi:

  • Cell wall made of chitin (1 mark)
  • Saprophytic nutrition / feed on dead organic matter (1 mark)
  • Do not have chloroplasts / cannot photosynthesise (1 mark)
  • Mostly multicellular (1 mark) (any two)

Prokaryotes:

  • No true nucleus / DNA free in cytoplasm (1 mark)
  • Unicellular (1 mark)
  • Cell wall not made of cellulose (1 mark) (any two)
Question 2 [3 marks]

Explain why plants and fungi are classified in different kingdoms, even though both have cell walls.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Plants have cell walls made of cellulose; fungi have cell walls made of chitin (1 mark)
  • Plants are autotrophic / make their own food by photosynthesis; fungi are saprophytic / heterotrophic (1 mark)
  • Plants have chloroplasts; fungi do not have chloroplasts (1 mark)
Question 3 [4 marks]

A student observes an unknown organism under a microscope. They can see that it is unicellular, has a nucleus, and has small hair-like structures (cilia) covering its surface.

(a) State which kingdom this organism most likely belongs to. [1 mark]

(b) Explain your answer, giving two reasons based on the features described. [2 marks]

(c) Name one example of an organism that could match this description. [1 mark]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Protoctists (1 mark)
  • (b) It is unicellular / single-celled (1 mark)
  • (b) It has a nucleus / is eukaryotic, which rules out prokaryotes (1 mark)
  • (c) Paramecium (1 mark) (also accept other ciliated protoctists)
Question 4 [3 marks]

Describe three features used by scientists to classify organisms into different groups.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Cell type - prokaryotic or eukaryotic (1 mark)
  • Cell structure - presence and type of cell wall (1 mark)
  • Number of cells - unicellular or multicellular (1 mark)
  • Nutrition method - autotrophic or heterotrophic (1 mark)
  • Body structure - presence of tissues, organs (1 mark)
  • Type of reproduction - sexual or asexual (1 mark) (any three)
Question 5 [4 marks]

Compare the features of organisms in the Animal kingdom with organisms in the Plant kingdom under the following headings:

(a) Cell wall [2 marks]

(b) Method of nutrition [2 marks]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER

(a) Cell wall:

  • Animals have no cell wall (1 mark)
  • Plants have a cell wall made of cellulose (1 mark)

(b) Method of nutrition:

  • Animals are heterotrophic / feed on other organisms (1 mark)
  • Plants are autotrophic / make their own food by photosynthesis (1 mark)
Question 6 [3 marks]

Explain why classification is important for scientists studying biodiversity.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • Allows scientists to communicate about organisms using standard / universal names (1 mark)
  • Shows evolutionary relationships between organisms (1 mark)
  • Helps identify new species and understand how many different species exist (1 mark)
  • Organises biological knowledge in a logical way (1 mark)
  • Assists in conservation efforts by understanding which species are related (1 mark) (any three)
Question 7 [6 marks]

The table below shows some features of five organisms labelled A to E.

Organism Cell Wall Nucleus Nutrition Cells
A Cellulose Yes Autotrophic Multicellular
B Chitin Yes Saprophytic Multicellular
C None Yes Heterotrophic Multicellular
D Present (not cellulose) No Varied Unicellular
E None Yes Heterotrophic Unicellular

Identify the kingdom to which each organism belongs. Give a reason for each answer.

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • A: Plant kingdom - has cellulose cell wall and is autotrophic (1 mark)
  • B: Fungi kingdom - has chitin cell wall and saprophytic nutrition (1 mark)
  • C: Animal kingdom - no cell wall, multicellular and heterotrophic (1 mark)
  • D: Prokaryote kingdom - no true nucleus, unicellular with cell wall (1 mark)
  • E: Protoctist kingdom - unicellular with a nucleus / eukaryotic (1 mark)
  • One additional mark for any fully justified answer with clear reasoning (1 mark)
