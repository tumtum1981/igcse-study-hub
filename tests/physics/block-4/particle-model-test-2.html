20 Multiple Choice Questions | The Particle Model

Question 1

What happens to the particles in a solid when it is heated?

A They get larger in size
B They slow down and stop moving
C They gain energy and vibrate more
D They move apart and form a regular pattern
Question 2

A The particles are far apart and can be pushed closer together
B The particles shrink when pressure is applied
C The particles are in a regular arrangement that can be rearranged
D The particles are destroyed when squeezed
Question 3

A The particles are very cold and cannot move at all
B The particles are far apart and move slowly
C The particles are randomly arranged but touching
D The particles are in fixed positions held by strong forces
Question 4

A Strongest of all three states
B Weaker than solid but stronger than gas
C Same as in a gas
D Weakest of all three states

A Water particles are already close together with very little space between them
B Water particles are in a fixed regular arrangement
C Water particles are too heavy to be pushed
D Water particles are bonded to the syringe walls

A Gas particles are arranged in a regular pattern
B Gas particles move quickly and randomly in all directions
C Gas particles are attracted to each other strongly
D Gas particles expand in size as they travel

A Solid, liquid, gas
B Gas, liquid, solid

B Particles in solids do not move at all

B Gas

B They lose energy, slow down, and move closer together
