Speed, Distance and Time Exam

Exam-style questions with full worked solutions

Exam Instructions

Answer all questions. Show all working for calculation questions. The total marks for this exam is 42 marks.

Key Formula: speed = distance / time
v = d / t

* Exam Questions

Question 1 [4 marks]

A cyclist travels 24 km in 1.5 hours.

(a) Calculate the average speed of the cyclist in km/h. [2]

(b) Convert this speed to m/s. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = distance / time [1 mark]
  • Speed = 24 / 1.5 = 16 km/h [1 mark]
  • (b) To convert km/h to m/s, divide by 3.6 [1 mark]
  • Speed = 16 / 3.6 = 4.44 m/s (or 4.4 m/s) [1 mark]
Question 2 [6 marks]

A car travels from town A to town B, a distance of 120 km, at an average speed of 60 km/h. It then travels from town B to town C, a distance of 80 km, at an average speed of 40 km/h.

(a) Calculate the time taken for each part of the journey. [2]

(b) Calculate the total time for the whole journey. [1]

(c) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey from A to C. [3]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Time A to B: t = d / v = 120 / 60 = 2 hours [1 mark]
  • Time B to C: t = d / v = 80 / 40 = 2 hours [1 mark]
  • (b) Total time = 2 + 2 = 4 hours [1 mark]
  • (c) Total distance = 120 + 80 = 200 km [1 mark]
  • Average speed = total distance / total time [1 mark]
  • Average speed = 200 / 4 = 50 km/h [1 mark]
Question 3 [5 marks]

A sprinter runs 100 m in 10 seconds, rests for 30 seconds, then runs another 100 m in 12 seconds.

(a) Calculate the sprinter's speed during the first 100 m. [2]

(b) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey (including rest time). [3]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = distance / time [1 mark]
  • Speed = 100 / 10 = 10 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) Total distance = 100 + 100 = 200 m [1 mark]
  • Total time = 10 + 30 + 12 = 52 s [1 mark]
  • Average speed = 200 / 52 = 3.85 m/s (or 3.8 m/s) [1 mark]
Question 4 [6 marks]

A train travels at a constant speed of 25 m/s.

(a) How far will the train travel in 3 minutes? [3]

(b) How long will it take the train to travel 15 km? Give your answer in minutes. [3]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Convert time: 3 minutes = 3 x 60 = 180 s [1 mark]
  • Distance = speed x time [1 mark]
  • Distance = 25 x 180 = 4500 m (or 4.5 km) [1 mark]
  • (b) Convert distance: 15 km = 15000 m [1 mark]
  • Time = distance / speed = 15000 / 25 = 600 s [1 mark]
  • Time = 600 / 60 = 10 minutes [1 mark]
Question 5 [5 marks]

(a) Define the term 'speed'. [1]

(b) Explain the difference between speed and velocity. [2]

(c) A car travels around a circular track at a constant speed of 20 m/s. Explain why the velocity is changing even though the speed is constant. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed is the distance travelled per unit time [1 mark]
  • (b) Speed is a scalar quantity (magnitude only) [1 mark]
  • Velocity is a vector quantity (has both magnitude and direction) [1 mark]
  • (c) Although the speed (magnitude) stays the same at 20 m/s [1 mark]
  • The direction is constantly changing as the car goes around the circle, so velocity changes [1 mark]
Question 6 [8 marks]

Sound travels at approximately 340 m/s in air. During a thunderstorm, a person sees a lightning flash and hears the thunder 4 seconds later.

(a) Calculate the distance from the person to the lightning strike. [2]

(b) Light travels at 3 x 10^8 m/s. Calculate the time taken for the light to travel this distance. [2]

(c) Explain why we can assume the light reaches us almost instantly compared to the sound. [2]

(d) If a second flash is seen and thunder is heard 7 seconds later, how much further away was this second lightning strike? [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Distance = speed x time [1 mark]
  • Distance = 340 x 4 = 1360 m [1 mark]
  • (b) Time = distance / speed = 1360 / (3 x 10^8) [1 mark]
  • Time = 4.53 x 10^-6 s (or 0.00000453 s) [1 mark]
  • (c) Light is approximately 880,000 times faster than sound [1 mark]
  • The time for light to travel any reasonable distance is negligible/too small to notice [1 mark]
  • (d) Second distance = 340 x 7 = 2380 m [1 mark]
  • Difference = 2380 - 1360 = 1020 m further away [1 mark]
Question 7 [8 marks]

A delivery driver makes three stops during a journey. The data is shown below:

Start to Stop 1: 15 km at 30 km/h

Stop 1 to Stop 2: 24 km at 48 km/h

Stop 2 to End: 18 km at 36 km/h

(a) Calculate the time taken for each section of the journey. [3]

(b) Calculate the total distance travelled. [1]

(c) Calculate the total time for the journey. [1]

(d) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey. [2]

(e) Explain why the average speed is not 38 km/h (the average of 30, 48, and 36). [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Time 1 = 15 / 30 = 0.5 hours [1 mark]
  • Time 2 = 24 / 48 = 0.5 hours [1 mark]
  • Time 3 = 18 / 36 = 0.5 hours [1 mark]
  • (b) Total distance = 15 + 24 + 18 = 57 km [1 mark]
  • (c) Total time = 0.5 + 0.5 + 0.5 = 1.5 hours [1 mark]
  • (d) Average speed = total distance / total time [1 mark]
  • Average speed = 57 / 1.5 = 38 km/h [1 mark]
  • (e) Average speed must be calculated from total distance / total time, not by averaging speeds (in this case the answer happens to equal 38 km/h because equal time was spent at each speed) [1 mark]
