Distance-Time Graphs Exam

Exam-style questions with full worked solutions

Exam Instructions

Answer all questions. Show all working for calculation questions. The total marks for this exam is 40 marks.

Key Formula: gradient = change in distance / change in time = speed
The gradient of a distance-time graph gives speed

* Exam Questions

Question 1 [6 marks]

A distance-time graph shows a cyclist's journey. The graph shows a straight line from the origin (0,0) to point A (20s, 100m), then a horizontal line to point B (40s, 100m), then a straight line to point C (60s, 250m).

(a) Calculate the speed during the first section (0 to A). [2]

(b) Describe the motion between A and B. [1]

(c) Calculate the speed during the final section (B to C). [2]

(d) Which section had the fastest speed? [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = gradient = change in distance / change in time [1 mark]
  • Speed = 100 / 20 = 5 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) The cyclist is stationary / at rest / not moving (horizontal line means zero gradient/speed) [1 mark]
  • (c) Speed = (250 - 100) / (60 - 40) [1 mark]
  • Speed = 150 / 20 = 7.5 m/s [1 mark]
  • (d) Section B to C (7.5 m/s is greater than 5 m/s) [1 mark]
Question 2 [5 marks]

(a) What does the gradient of a distance-time graph represent? [1]

(b) What does a horizontal line on a distance-time graph indicate about the object's motion? [1]

(c) What does a curved line that gets steeper on a distance-time graph indicate? [2]

(d) Explain why the line on a distance-time graph cannot go downwards. [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) The gradient represents the speed of the object [1 mark]
  • (b) The object is stationary / not moving / at rest [1 mark]
  • (c) The object is accelerating / speeding up [1 mark]
  • The gradient is increasing, meaning the speed is increasing [1 mark]
  • (d) Distance-time graphs show total distance travelled, which can only increase or stay the same, never decrease (for displacement-time graphs, the line can go down) [1 mark]
Question 3 [7 marks]

Two students, A and B, start a race at the same time. Student A runs at a constant speed and reaches 80 m in 10 seconds. Student B starts slowly and accelerates throughout the race.

(a) Calculate student A's speed. [2]

(b) Describe how the distance-time graphs for the two students would differ. [3]

(c) At t = 5 seconds, student B has only travelled 15 m. Calculate student A's lead at this point. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = distance / time [1 mark]
  • Speed = 80 / 10 = 8 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) Student A: straight diagonal line (constant gradient) [1 mark]
  • Student B: curved line that gets steeper [1 mark]
  • Both lines start at the origin; B's line starts with a gentler gradient than A's [1 mark]
  • (c) Student A's distance at 5s = 8 x 5 = 40 m [1 mark]
  • Lead = 40 - 15 = 25 m ahead [1 mark]
Question 4 [6 marks]

A car's journey is recorded on a distance-time graph with the following coordinates: (0s, 0m), (10s, 200m), (25s, 200m), (35s, 500m).

(a) Calculate the speed during the first 10 seconds. [2]

(b) For how long was the car stationary? [1]

(c) Calculate the speed during the final section. [2]

(d) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey. [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = gradient = 200 / 10 [1 mark]
  • Speed = 20 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) Stationary from 10s to 25s = 15 seconds [1 mark]
  • (c) Speed = (500 - 200) / (35 - 25) = 300 / 10 [1 mark]
  • Speed = 30 m/s [1 mark]
  • (d) Average speed = 500 / 35 = 14.3 m/s [1 mark]
Question 5 [8 marks]

A delivery van travels between three locations. The distance-time data is:

Section 1: Travels 600 m in 30 s

Section 2: Stationary for 60 s

Section 3: Travels 900 m in 45 s

(a) Calculate the speed during section 1. [2]

(b) What would section 2 look like on a distance-time graph? [1]

(c) Calculate the speed during section 3. [2]

(d) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey. [3]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Speed = distance / time [1 mark]
  • Speed = 600 / 30 = 20 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) A horizontal line (gradient = 0) [1 mark]
  • (c) Speed = 900 / 45 [1 mark]
  • Speed = 20 m/s [1 mark]
  • (d) Total distance = 600 + 0 + 900 = 1500 m [1 mark]
  • Total time = 30 + 60 + 45 = 135 s [1 mark]
  • Average speed = 1500 / 135 = 11.1 m/s [1 mark]
Question 6 [8 marks]

Two cars, X and Y, travel along the same road. Car X travels at a constant speed of 15 m/s. Car Y starts 100 m behind car X at t = 0 and travels at a constant speed of 20 m/s.

(a) Write an equation for the distance travelled by car X from the starting point of car Y, in terms of time t. [1]

(b) Write an equation for the distance travelled by car Y in terms of time t. [1]

(c) At what time will car Y catch up with car X? [3]

(d) How far will each car have travelled from car Y's starting point when they meet? [2]

(e) Describe how you could show this graphically on a distance-time graph. [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Distance X = 100 + 15t (starting 100 m ahead) [1 mark]
  • (b) Distance Y = 20t [1 mark]
  • (c) When they meet: 100 + 15t = 20t [1 mark]
  • 100 = 20t - 15t = 5t [1 mark]
  • t = 100 / 5 = 20 seconds [1 mark]
  • (d) Car Y: distance = 20 x 20 = 400 m [1 mark]
  • Car X: distance = 100 + (15 x 20) = 400 m (same, as expected) [1 mark]
  • (e) Plot both lines on the same graph; the intersection point shows when and where they meet [1 mark]
