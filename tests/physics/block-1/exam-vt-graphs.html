Velocity-Time Graphs Exam

Exam-style questions with full worked solutions

Exam Instructions

Answer all questions. Show all working for calculation questions. The total marks for this exam is 44 marks.

Key Formulas: gradient = acceleration, area under graph = distance
a = (v - u) / t

* Exam Questions

Question 1 [6 marks]

A car accelerates uniformly from rest to 30 m/s in 10 seconds, then travels at constant velocity for 20 seconds.

(a) Calculate the acceleration during the first 10 seconds. [2]

(b) Calculate the distance travelled during the acceleration phase. [2]

(c) Calculate the total distance travelled in 30 seconds. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration = (v - u) / t = (30 - 0) / 10 [1 mark]
  • Acceleration = 3 m/s^2 [1 mark]
  • (b) Distance = area of triangle = 1/2 x base x height [1 mark]
  • Distance = 1/2 x 10 x 30 = 150 m [1 mark]
  • (c) Distance at constant velocity = 30 x 20 = 600 m [1 mark]
  • Total distance = 150 + 600 = 750 m [1 mark]
Question 2 [7 marks]

A train travelling at 25 m/s applies its brakes and decelerates uniformly, coming to rest in 50 seconds.

(a) Calculate the deceleration of the train. [2]

(b) Calculate the braking distance using the area under the velocity-time graph. [2]

(c) The train has a mass of 80,000 kg. Calculate the braking force. [3]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Deceleration = (v - u) / t = (0 - 25) / 50 [1 mark]
  • Deceleration = -0.5 m/s^2 (or 0.5 m/s^2 deceleration) [1 mark]
  • (b) Distance = area of triangle = 1/2 x 50 x 25 [1 mark]
  • Distance = 625 m [1 mark]
  • (c) Using F = ma [1 mark]
  • F = 80000 x 0.5 [1 mark]
  • F = 40000 N (or 40 kN) [1 mark]
Question 3 [5 marks]

(a) What does the gradient of a velocity-time graph represent? [1]

(b) What does a horizontal line on a velocity-time graph indicate? [1]

(c) What does the area under a velocity-time graph represent? [1]

(d) Explain the difference between what a horizontal line means on a distance-time graph compared to a velocity-time graph. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration [1 mark]
  • (b) Constant velocity / zero acceleration [1 mark]
  • (c) Distance travelled / displacement [1 mark]
  • (d) On a distance-time graph, a horizontal line means the object is stationary (not moving) [1 mark]
  • On a velocity-time graph, a horizontal line means constant velocity (moving at steady speed) [1 mark]
Question 4 [8 marks]

A velocity-time graph shows the following journey of a motorcycle:

0-8 s: velocity increases from 0 to 24 m/s

8-20 s: constant velocity of 24 m/s

20-26 s: velocity decreases from 24 m/s to 0

(a) Calculate the acceleration during the first 8 seconds. [2]

(b) Calculate the deceleration during the final phase. [2]

(c) Calculate the total distance travelled. [4]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration = (24 - 0) / 8 [1 mark]
  • Acceleration = 3 m/s^2 [1 mark]
  • (b) Deceleration = (0 - 24) / (26 - 20) = -24 / 6 [1 mark]
  • Deceleration = -4 m/s^2 (or 4 m/s^2) [1 mark]
  • (c) Distance 1 (triangle) = 1/2 x 8 x 24 = 96 m [1 mark]
  • Distance 2 (rectangle) = 12 x 24 = 288 m [1 mark]
  • Distance 3 (triangle) = 1/2 x 6 x 24 = 72 m [1 mark]
  • Total distance = 96 + 288 + 72 = 456 m [1 mark]
Question 5 [6 marks]

A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 6 seconds.

(a) Calculate the acceleration. [2]

(b) Calculate the distance travelled during this acceleration using the area under the v-t graph (trapezium). [3]

(c) State the shape formed under the v-t graph during this motion. [1]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Acceleration = (v - u) / t = (25 - 10) / 6 [1 mark]
  • Acceleration = 2.5 m/s^2 [1 mark]
  • (b) Area of trapezium = 1/2 x (a + b) x h [1 mark]
  • Area = 1/2 x (10 + 25) x 6 [1 mark]
  • Distance = 1/2 x 35 x 6 = 105 m [1 mark]
  • (c) Trapezium [1 mark]
Question 6 [6 marks]

A cyclist and a car start from the same point at the same time. The cyclist accelerates uniformly from rest to 8 m/s in 4 seconds, then continues at constant velocity. The car accelerates uniformly from rest to 20 m/s in 10 seconds.

(a) Calculate the acceleration of the cyclist. [1]

(b) Calculate the acceleration of the car. [1]

(c) Calculate the distance travelled by the cyclist in the first 10 seconds. [2]

(d) Calculate the distance travelled by the car in the first 10 seconds. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) Cyclist acceleration = 8 / 4 = 2 m/s^2 [1 mark]
  • (b) Car acceleration = 20 / 10 = 2 m/s^2 [1 mark]
  • (c) Cyclist: triangle (0-4s) = 1/2 x 4 x 8 = 16 m [1 mark]
  • Plus rectangle (4-10s) = 8 x 6 = 48 m; Total = 64 m [1 mark]
  • (d) Car: triangle = 1/2 x 10 x 20 [1 mark]
  • Distance = 100 m [1 mark]
Question 7 [6 marks]

A ball is dropped from rest and falls freely under gravity (g = 10 m/s^2, ignore air resistance) for 3 seconds.

(a) Calculate the velocity of the ball after 3 seconds. [2]

(b) Sketch a velocity-time graph for this motion. [2]

(c) Calculate the distance fallen using the area under your graph. [2]

* CLICK TO REVEAL ANSWER
  • (a) v = u + at = 0 + (10 x 3) [1 mark]
  • v = 30 m/s [1 mark]
  • (b) Graph should show: straight line from origin (0,0) to (3s, 30 m/s) [1 mark]
  • Positive gradient, axes correctly labelled [1 mark]
  • (c) Distance = area of triangle = 1/2 x 3 x 30 [1 mark]
  • Distance = 45 m [1 mark]
