Physics Formulas

v = d / t
Speed
v = speed (m/s) d = distance (m) t = time (s)
Block 1
a = (v - u) / t
Acceleration
a = acceleration (m/s²) v = final velocity (m/s) u = initial velocity (m/s) t = time (s)
Block 1
W = m × g
Weight
W = weight (N) m = mass (kg) g = gravitational field strength (N/kg)
Block 1
W = F × d
Work Done
W = work done (J) F = force (N) d = distance (m)
Block 1
ρ = m / V
Density
ρ = density (kg/m³ or g/cm³) m = mass (kg or g) V = volume (m³ or cm³)
Block 2
M = F × d
Moment
M = moment (Nm) F = force (N) d = perpendicular distance from pivot (m)
Block 2
F = m × a
Newton's Second Law
F = resultant force (N) m = mass (kg) a = acceleration (m/s²)
Block 2
GPE = m × g × h
Gravitational Potential Energy
GPE = energy (J) m = mass (kg) g = gravitational field strength (N/kg) h = height (m)
Block 3
KE = ½ × m × v²
Kinetic Energy
KE = kinetic energy (J) m = mass (kg) v = velocity (m/s)

Chemistry Formulas

Mass of reactants = Mass of products
Conservation of Mass
In a closed system, total mass before = total mass after Atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed
Block 3
Relative Atomic Mass (Ar)
Ar Calculation
Ar = weighted average mass of isotopes Compared to 1/12 of carbon-12
Block 1
Ion charge = protons - electrons
Ion Charge
Positive ion: lost electrons (more protons) Negative ion: gained electrons (more electrons)

Formula Tips

  • Rearranging formulas: Use the formula triangle method - cover what you want to find
  • Units matter: Always check your units match (e.g., km to m, minutes to seconds)
  • Show working: Write the formula, substitute values, then calculate
  • Significant figures: Give your answer to the same number of significant figures as the data
